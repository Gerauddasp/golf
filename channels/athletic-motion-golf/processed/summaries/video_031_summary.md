## Overview

This short video addresses a critical misconception about hip rotation in the golf swing. Many golfers are being taught to rotate around the trail (right) hip during the downswing, which keeps them too far from the ball and prevents proper weight transfer. Athletic Motion Golf explains the biomechanically correct rotation pattern: the pelvis should rotate around its CENTER in both the backswing and downswing, creating a dynamic movement where the hips move both away from and toward the ball.

## Key Concepts

### Correct Pelvis Rotation Mechanics

**Backswing Rotation**
- Pelvis rotates around its center point (not around the lead or trail hip)
- From a bird's-eye view, the pelvis closes/rotates away from the target
- The right side of pelvis moves back, left side moves forward
- This creates movement AWAY from the ball

**Downswing Rotation**
- Pelvis continues to rotate around its center (not around the trail hip)
- The left side of pelvis moves back, right side moves forward
- This creates movement TOWARD the ball
- Hips naturally shift back out toward the target line

### Common Misconception (Dead Wrong)

**The Flawed Teaching**
- Players are being told to rotate around the TRAIL HIP during the downswing
- This keeps the pelvis stuck back and away from the ball
- Nothing moves back toward the target line
- Results in loss of power, poor sequencing, and inconsistent contact

**Why It's Problematic**
- Violates natural biomechanics observed in elite players
- Prevents proper weight transfer and pressure shift
- Keeps center of mass too far behind the ball at impact
- Limits hip rotation speed and efficiency

## Biomechanical Principles

### Center-Axis Rotation

**The Correct Model**
- Think of the pelvis rotating around a vertical axis through its center
- Like a door rotating on hinges placed in the middle, not on one side
- Both backswing and downswing use the same center-axis rotation
- Creates reciprocal movement: away from ball (backswing), toward ball (downswing)

**Bird's-Eye View Visual**
- Imagine looking down at the pelvis from above
- Right hip and left hip are equal distances from center axis
- Backswing: pelvis rotates clockwise (for right-handed golfer)
- Downswing: pelvis rotates counter-clockwise around same center point

### Dynamic Hip Movement Pattern

**Three-Dimensional Motion**
- Rotation: around the center axis
- Translation: toward target in downswing
- Relationship: rotation and translation happen simultaneously
- Result: hips move both rotationally and laterally toward the ball

## Actionable Corrections

### Feel and Awareness Drill

**Purpose**: Develop awareness of center-axis rotation vs. trail-hip rotation

**Setup**
- Stand in golf posture without a club
- Place hands on hips to feel pelvic movement
- Focus on sensing the center point of your pelvis

**Execution**
1. Make a backswing turn, feeling pelvis rotate around its center
2. Notice right hip moving back, left hip moving forward
3. In transition, feel the reversal
4. Downswing: left hip moves back, right hip moves forward
5. Key feel: right hip should move TOWARD the ball in downswing

**Success Markers**
- You feel your right hip approaching the ball in downswing
- Weight naturally shifts toward target
- No sensation of "hanging back" or staying on trail side

### Mirror Work - Top-Down View

**Purpose**: Visual confirmation of proper rotation axis

**Setup**
- Use phone camera or mirror positioned above you
- Set up recording from directly above (bird's-eye view)
- Make slow-motion swings

**Execution**
1. Record your swing from above
2. Draw a vertical line through center of your pelvis at address
3. Watch backswing: pelvis should rotate around that center line
4. Watch downswing: pelvis should rotate around SAME center line
5. Check: right hip should move forward toward ball in downswing

**Success Markers**
- Pelvis rotates symmetrically around center axis
- No "fixed" trail hip acting as rotation point
- Clear movement of right hip toward ball in downswing

## Key Takeaways

1. **Center-Axis Rotation is Fundamental** - The pelvis rotates around its center in both backswing and downswing, not around either hip

2. **Common Teaching is Biomechanically Wrong** - Rotating around the trail hip in the downswing violates natural movement patterns and keeps golfers too far from the ball

3. **Reciprocal Movement Pattern** - Backswing moves pelvis away from ball, downswing moves it back toward ball - both rotating around the same center axis

4. **Right Hip Must Move Forward** - In the downswing, the right hip (trail hip) should move toward the ball and target line, not stay back

5. **Elite Players All Do This** - 3D biomechanical data shows all elite players rotate around the pelvis center, not around one hip
