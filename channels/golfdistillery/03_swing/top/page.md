# Top of the Swing - How to Set your Golf Club Correctly at the Top

[Swing Tips for the Top of the Swing][images/swing-tips-top-of-the-swing.jpg]

The top of the golf swing occurs right after the backswing and is followed by the downswing. Follow these tips for a proper top of the swing position.

## Key Top of Swing Checkpoints

### 1. Left Wrist Position

[Left Wrist Flat][images/top-left-wrist.png]

The wrist should be "fully hinged" without bowing or cupping. "The top of the left hand lines up with the rest of the forearms and upper arm."

### 2. Shaft Alignment

[Shaft Points at Target][images/top-shaft.png]

"The shaft of the club should be pointing straight ahead, at the target" or parallel to the target lineâ€”avoiding laid-off or across-the-line positions.

### 3. Shaft Level

[Shaft Parallel to Ground][images/top-shaftlevel.png]

"Hands should stop rising when the shaft reaches the point where it is parallel to the ground, or just shy of that position."

### 4. Spine Angle

[Maintain Spine Angle][images/backswing-spine.png]

"The correct spine angle was determined while setting up for a golf shot at address and that spine angle should be kept intact during the backswing."

### 5. Back Positioning

[Back Faces Target][images/top-back.png]

"Your back should be facing the target" through proper hip and shoulder rotation.

### 6. Wrist Hinging

[Full Wrist Hinge][images/backswing-hinge.png]

"Wrists should be fully hinged" by the end of the backswing.
