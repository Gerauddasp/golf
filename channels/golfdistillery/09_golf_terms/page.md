# Golf Terms: Illustrated Definitions

[Golf Terms][images/golf-terms.jpg]

The page presents a comprehensive resource for understanding golf terminology, organized into 13 distinct categories with visual guides and detailed explanations.

---

## 1. Types of Golf Clubs

[Types of golf clubs][images/club-types.jpg]

Golfers may carry up to 14 clubs, selecting their preferred combination. This section covers woods (driver and fairway woods), hybrids, irons, wedges (pitching, gap, sand, and lob wedges), and putters.

---

## 2. Parts of a Golf Club

[Terms of parts of a golf club][images/club-parts.jpg]

This category explains specific club components including the grip, shaft, and clubhead. Detailed parts covered are the hosel, ferrule, sole, heel, clubface, grind, bounce, leading edge, loft, and lie.

---

## 3. Golf Setup Terms

[Setup terms in golf][images/setup.jpg]

Before executing a shot, golfers position their body relative to the ball, club, and target. Key concepts include setup positioning (grip, feet position, ball position), aim, alignment, and target line.

---

## 4. Golf Swing Terms

[Swing terms in golf][images/swing.jpg]

The swing encompasses a choreographed sequence beginning with setup and ending after the ball's flight. Sections include pre-shot routine, address, takeaway, backswing, top of swing, downswing, impact, release, follow-through, swing plane, and timing.

---

## 5. Swing Errors

[Swing error terms in golf][images/swing-errors.jpg]

Complex movements can result in deviations from textbook form. Common errors explained include swaying, yips, and reverse pivot.

---

## 6. Ball Flights

[The different flight paths a golf ball can take][images/ball-flights-1-300x300.png]

From the moment it is struck to the moment when it finally lands a golf ball can travel along a variety of ball flights. Terms covered include draw, fade, slice, hook, push, and pull.

---

## 7. Types of Golf Shots

[Types of golf shots][images/shot-types.jpg]

Golfers must master numerous shot variations using different clubs based on ball position and environmental factors. Both long game (drive, approach) and short game (pitch, chip, flop, bunker shot, putt) shots are included.

---

## 8. Golf Shot Errors

[Terms of shot errors in golf][images/shot-errors.jpg]

Reducing mishits improves scoring and handicap. Terms explained include double tap, fat, whiff, thin (top), and shank.

---

## 9. Shot Lies

[Terms of shot lies in golf][images/shot-lies.jpg]

Ball location determines shot difficulty throughout the round. Terms covered include plugged, tight, fluffy, buried, uphill, downhill, and fried-egg lies.

---

## 10. Handicap Golf Terms

[Handicap terms in golf][images/handicap.jpg]

Since courses vary significantly, standardized comparison requires additional factors. Concepts explained include scratch vs. bogey golfers, course rating, slope rating, course handicap, and handicap index.

---

## 11. Game Formats

[Play format terms in golf][images/formats.jpg]

Contrary to common assumption, golf features multiple playing formats beyond the standard televised version. At least fourteen formats exist, including scramble, match play, best ball, four ball, medal, skins, and stableford.

---

## 12. Golf Scoring Terms

[Scoring terms in golf][images/scoring.jpg]

The ultimate objective in a traditional game of golf is to finish a round of 18 holes using the least amount of strokes. Named scores include bogey, birdie, par, eagle, albatross (double eagle), and double bogey.

---

## 13. Course Elements

[Terms of elements of a golf course][images/course-elements.jpg]

Golf courses contain diverse areas and objects encountered during play. Terms cover the teeing ground, tee markers, fairway, rough, fringe, cup, flagstick, bunker types (green-side, fairway, waste), ground under repair, and out of bounds.
