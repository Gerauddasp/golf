# Bunker Shots: Tips on How to Hit from Any Bunker

This section offers tips on how to hit golf shots from sand bunkers. Bunker shots covered include from wet sand, plugged lie, fried-egg lie, upslope, downslope, long, short and finally fairway bunker shots.

---

## 1. Regular Bunker Shot - **Start Here!**

**Overview:**
The basics to the typical bunker shots are covered and a lot of the tips will apply to other bunker variations. This serves as the foundational section for all greenside bunker play.

**Key Fundamentals:**
- Use sand wedge with bounce
- Hit sand 2 inches behind ball
- Open clubface and stance
- Accelerate through the sand
- Complete follow-through

## 2. Wet Sand (Compact Sand)

**The Challenge:**
Addresses bunkers with wet or compacted sand conditions.

**Problem:**
The club can bounce from the hard surface and shoot your ball clean over the green without proper technique adjustments.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Use lob wedge instead of sand wedge
- Aim 1 inch behind ball (not 2 inches)
- More precise contact required
- Reduce bounce engagement

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit from Wet/Compact Sand](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-a-bunker-shot-wet-sand/)

## 3. Plugged Lie

**The Challenge:**
Focuses on situations where your golf ball lands into a firm bunker and digs in rather than bounce off.

**Goal:**
Successfully dislodge the ball from its tricky buried location.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Square the clubface (not open)
- Aim 3 inches behind ball
- Steeper angle of attack
- Aggressive swing required
- Expect less spin and more roll

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit from a Plugged Lie](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-a-bunker-shot-plugged/)

## 4. Fried-Egg Lie

**The Challenge:**
Describes scenarios where your golf ball lands into a fluffy bunker and stays inside the crater it created.

**Goal:**
Avoid hitting the ball thin or leaving the ball in the bunker.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Special setup for crater conditions
- Focus on center contact
- Manage fluffy sand texture
- Control trajectory

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit from a Fried-Egg Lie](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-a-bunker-shot-fried-egg/)

## 5. Upslope (Uphill)

**The Challenge:**
Covers shots where the ball lands or rolls up the side of the bunker closest to the green.

**Problem:**
Without modifications, your club will dig into the sand behind the ball and get stuck there.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Match spine angle to slope
- Ball more forward in stance
- Swing along the slope
- Expect higher trajectory
- Ball will fly shorter

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit from an Upslope](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-a-bunker-shot-upslope/)

## 6. Downslope (Downhill)

**The Challenge:**
Addresses balls resting up the side of the bunker farthest away from the green.

**Problem:**
Without adjustments, you risk skulling the ball thin and sending it over the green.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Match spine angle to slope
- Ball back in stance
- Swing along the slope
- Expect lower trajectory
- Ball will run more
- Most difficult bunker shot

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit from a Downslope](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-a-bunker-shot-downslope/)

## 7. Long Bunker Shot

**The Challenge:**
Applies when the ball sits far from the flag on a large green.

**Goal:**
Send the ball up onto the green and rolling towards the hole.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Longer backswing for distance
- Maintain acceleration
- Still hit sand first (not ball)
- May need to contact closer to ball (1.5" vs 2")
- Consider club selection (pitching wedge vs sand wedge)

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit a Long Bunker Shot](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-long-bunker-shot/)

## 8. Short Bunker Shot

**The Challenge:**
Focuses on situations with minimal green space between bunker and hole.

**Goal:**
Make the ball fly a short distance and land softly onto the green with minimal roll.

**Key Adjustments:**
- Shorter backswing (half swing)
- Maintain full acceleration
- Very open clubface
- Maximum loft
- Soft landing required

**Link to Detailed Guide:**
[How to Hit a Short Bunker Shot](https://www.golfdistillery.com/shot-tips/bunker-shots/how-to-hit-short-bunker-shot/)

## 9. Fairway Bunker

**The Challenge:**
Addresses bunkers located in the fairway or beyond greenside areas.

**Important Note:**
The advice for greenside bunkers presented above do not apply since increased distance requirements change the fundamentals completely.

**Key Differences:**
- Hit ball first (not sand first)
- Use less lofted club for distance
- Clean contact essential
- Focus on getting out and advancing ball
- Club selection based on lip height

## Learning Path Recommendation

**For Beginners:**
1. Start with Regular Bunker Shot - master the fundamentals
2. Practice on level lies with normal sand
3. Build confidence with basic technique
4. Then progress to specialty shots

**For Intermediate Players:**
1. Review Regular Bunker Shot technique
2. Add Wet Sand and Long/Short shots to your repertoire
3. Practice different distances
4. Develop feel for various sand conditions

**For Advanced Players:**
1. Master all nine bunker shot types
2. Practice from difficult lies regularly
3. Develop ability to assess and adapt quickly
4. Build confidence in any bunker situation

---

## Practice Tips

- Spend time in practice bunkers regularly
- Start with basic shots before attempting difficult lies
- Draw lines in sand to practice contact point
- Experiment with different clubs and techniques
- Practice various distances and trajectories
- Build confidence through repetition
- Always practice with full acceleration
