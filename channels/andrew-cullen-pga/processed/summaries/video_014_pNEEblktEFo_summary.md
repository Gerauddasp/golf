## Main Topic: Balance Point and Weight Distribution

### Core Concept
- **Balance point** refers to where the pressure is distributed under your feet during the golf stance
- Proper weight distribution is essential for maintaining stable posture throughout the swing
- Even with perfect posture (steps 1 and 2), incorrect weight distribution will cause compensatory body movements

---

## Key Problems Addressed

### 1. Weight Too Far Forward (Into the Toes)
**Problem:**
- When weight shifts too much into the toes, the body becomes unstable
- The body's natural survival instinct kicks in to prevent falling over

**Consequence:**
- The body will react by standing up during the swing
- This changes your distance from the ball
- Makes it extremely difficult to make solid contact on the way down
- Results in inconsistent strikes

### 2. Weight Too Far Back (Into the Heels)
**Problem:**
- Sitting back in the heels too much creates backward instability
- Even with good posture, the body senses the imbalance

**Consequence:**
- The body reacts by moving forward during the swing
- This brings you closer to the ball than you started
- Increases the risk of "chunking" the ball (hitting behind it)
- Disrupts the swing path and contact point

---

## The Solution: Proper Balance Point

### Primary Balance Position
- **Place pressure underneath the balls of your feet**
- Feel the weight at the front of your shoelaces
- This neutral position prevents the body from having to react to balance issues
- Allows for stable posture from setup through impact

### Benefits of Proper Balance
- Body doesn't feel threatened by losing balance
- No compensatory movements needed during the swing
- Easy to maintain posture consistency from start to strike
- Distance to the ball remains constant throughout the swing

---

## Advanced Technique: Center of Gravity Control

### The Belt Buckle Reference Point
- Think of the point **behind your belt buckle**
- This represents your **center of mass** or **center of gravity**
- The key is to keep this point as stable as possible from start to impact

### Why This Matters
- Keeping your center of gravity stable maintains consistent distance to the ball
- The more stable this point remains, the better your chances of solid contact
- Creates a reliable foundation for the entire swing motion

## Memorable Teaching Analogy

### The Primitivo Wine Glass Visualization
Andrew uses a vivid mental image to help students understand stability:
- **Imagine a glass of beautiful primitivo wine sitting in the middle of your stomach**
- **Try not to spill a drop as you're swinging the club**

**Purpose of this visualization:**
- Makes the abstract concept of center of gravity concrete and relatable
- Provides a simple focus point during practice
- Encourages smooth, controlled movement rather than jerky motions
- Easy to remember and implement on the course

## Summary Checklist

To achieve proper balance point in your golf stance:

1. Establish your beautiful posture (from steps 1 and 2)
2. Distribute weight underneath the balls of your feet
3. Feel pressure at the front of your shoelaces
4. Avoid leaning into your toes or sitting back in your heels
5. Keep your center of gravity (belt buckle point) stable throughout the swing
6. Visualize not spilling that glass of wine from your stomach

---

## The Body's Natural Response to Imbalance

### Key Principle
**"The body never wants to fall over"**

This fundamental survival instinct means:
- Any imbalance will trigger automatic compensatory movements
- These movements are involuntary and difficult to control consciously
- They will override your intended swing mechanics
- Prevention (proper setup) is easier than correction (mid-swing)

### Why This Understanding Matters
- Explains why swing thoughts alone can't fix balance issues
- Highlights the importance of proper setup before swinging
- Shows that many swing faults originate from poor balance at address
- Emphasizes that the foundation (stance) must be correct for everything else to work

---

## Practical Application

### Practice Steps
1. Set up with proper posture
2. Rock gently forward and back to feel the difference between toes, heels, and balls of feet
3. Settle into the balls of your feet position
4. Make slow practice swings while maintaining that balance point
5. Use the wine glass visualization to check for excessive movement
6. Gradually increase swing speed while maintaining stability

### On-Course Implementation
- Check your balance point during your pre-shot routine
- If you're hitting fat or thin, reassess where your weight is positioned
- Remember: proper balance allows your body to stay stable naturally
- Don't fight against your body's balance instinctsâ€”set up correctly instead

## Key Takeaways

1. **Balance point is the third critical element** of proper golf stance fundamentals
2. **Weight should be on the balls of your feet**, not toes or heels
3. **Poor balance creates compensatory movements** that ruin your swing
4. **The body automatically reacts to imbalance**, making conscious correction difficult
5. **Stable center of gravity** (belt buckle point) is essential for consistent ball striking
6. **Prevention through proper setup** is more effective than mid-swing corrections
7. The **wine glass visualization** provides a memorable way to maintain stability
