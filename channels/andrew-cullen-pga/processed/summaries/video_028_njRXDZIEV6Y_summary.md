## Overview

Andrew Cullen challenges the traditional "one-size-fits-all" grip instruction and teaches golfers how to find their natural left hand grip position based on their unique anatomy and physiology. This personalized approach acknowledges that not everyone should follow the textbook "two knuckles showing" guideline.

---

## Key Concepts

### 1. Individual Variation in Grip Strength
- **Traditional teaching:** Two knuckles visible, V pointing to right shoulder
- **Reality:** Professional golfers use varying grip strengths successfully
- **Examples cited:**
  - Matthew Fitzpatrick: Super strong grip (approximately 4 knuckles)
  - Dustin Johnson: Super strong grip (approximately 4 knuckles)
  - Bernhard Langer: Strong grip in his era

### 2. Finding Your Natural Left Hand Position

**The Method:**
1. Stand straight up
2. Let both arms hang naturally at your sides
3. Observe how the hands hang naturally (they turn inward, not parallel to the body)
4. Place club on the ground, aimed at target
5. Wrap your left hand fingers around the club in this natural hanging position

**Result:** This reveals your individual natural grip position, which could be:
- 2 knuckles showing
- 2.5 knuckles showing
- 3 knuckles showing
- 4 knuckles showing ("gorilla hands")

### 3. The Biomechanics of Arm Rotation

**Critical Principle:** "Where the left forearm starts is where the left forearm wants to return"

- The left hand grip position directly affects arm rotation through impact
- Arm rotation controls the clubface angle at impact
- Using an unnatural grip forces compensations

---

## Common Grip Problems and Their Effects

### Problem #1: Too Weak (Unnatural for Your Anatomy)
**What happens:**
- Arm rotates clockwise through impact to return to natural position
- Clubface opens at impact
- Requires compensations to start ball on target
- Results in inconsistent ball striking

### Problem #2: Too Strong (Unnatural for Your Anatomy)
**What happens:**
- Arm over-rotates counterclockwise through impact
- Clubface closes at impact
- Ball starts left of target
- Requires compensations for accuracy

---

## The Importance of Natural Rotation

**Why it matters:**
- Natural grip = Natural rotation = Square clubface at impact
- Unnatural grip = Forced compensations = Inconsistent results
- The body wants to return the forearm to its natural position
- Fighting this natural tendency creates timing issues and inconsistency

## Step-by-Step Application

1. **Test Phase:**
   - Stand upright, arms hanging naturally
   - Notice the natural inward turn of the hands

2. **Setup Phase:**
   - Place club on ground, aimed at target
   - Maintain the natural hand position
   - Wrap fingers around grip in this position

3. **Verification:**
   - Check how many knuckles are visible
   - This is YOUR natural position (may differ from textbook)

4. **Practice:**
   - Use this grip consistently
   - Allow natural arm rotation
   - Minimize compensations
