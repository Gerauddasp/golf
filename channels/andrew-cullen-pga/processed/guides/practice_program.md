## QUICK START GUIDE

### How to Use This Program:

1. **Choose your session length:** 30, 60, or 90 minutes
2. **Follow the warm-up routine** - ALWAYS start here (10 min)
3. **Pick 2-3 practice stations** based on your focus area
4. **Complete drills in order** - beginner ‚Üí intermediate ‚Üí advanced
5. **Track your progress** - note what worked and what needs work

### Key Principles to Remember:

- **Quality over quantity** - Better to do 10 perfect reps than 50 sloppy ones
- **One thought at a time** - Andrew says men can only think of one thing during swing
- **Natural movement** - Work with your body, not against it
- **Feel before mechanics** - Get the feel first, then trust it

## WARM-UP ROUTINE (10 MINUTES)

**PURPOSE:** Wake up your body, establish feel, check fundamentals

**DO THIS EVERY SESSION - NO EXCEPTIONS**

---

### PHASE 1: Setup Check (3 minutes)

**No ball needed - just club in hand**

#### A. Grip Check (1 min)
1. **Find your natural grip:**
   - Stand straight, let left arm hang naturally
   - Notice how hand naturally rotates inward
   - Place club in hand in this natural position
   - **Check:** Can you see 2-4 knuckles? (Your natural amount)

2. **Thumb position:**
   - Use SHORT thumb position (Andrew's preference)
   - Thumb sits on top, not extended down

3. **Grip pressure:**
   - Level 3 on 1-5 scale
   - Soft enough to allow rotation, firm enough for control
   - **Advanced:** Try left hand = 4, right hand = 2

#### B. Posture Check (2 min)
1. **Mike Adams posture test:**
   - Stand straight with good posture
   - Place hands on thighs
   - Sit back and bend from hip joint
   - Slide hands down until longest finger touches TOP of kneecap
   - Feel tension in glutes
   - Let arms hang naturally

2. **Balance check:**
   - Feel pressure under balls of feet (shoelace area)
   - NOT in toes, NOT in heels
   - Stable, athletic position

3. **Center of gravity:**
   - Imagine glass of wine at belt buckle
   - Goal: Don't spill a drop during swing

**SUCCESS METRIC:** Position feels athletic and stable, like you could hold it comfortably for 30 seconds

---

### PHASE 2: Body Wake-Up (3 minutes)

**Now pick up your club**

#### A. Chest Rotation Drill (10 reps)
1. Take your posture position (no ball)
2. Pick a rib on your RIGHT side
3. Stretch that rib as FAR away as possible on backswing
4. Let arms swing WITH body (stay passive)
5. Make 10 slow-motion practice swings

**FEEL:** Your whole rib cage turning, not just shoulders

#### B. Feet Together Swings (10 reps)
1. Put feet together
2. Make half-speed swings (no ball)
3. Feel centralized position
4. Notice how body stays stable

**FEEL:** Can't move too far off center or you'll lose balance

#### C. Game Face Practice (5 reps)
1. Make practice swings with mouth SLIGHTLY open
2. No tension in jaw
3. Feel shoulders and arms soften

**FEEL:** Relaxed upper body, natural sequencing

---

### PHASE 3: Ball Contact Wake-Up (4 minutes)

#### A. Short Pitch Shots (10 balls)
- Use pitching wedge
- Targets 20-30 yards
- Focus ONLY on making clean contact
- Don't worry about where ball goes
- Just wake up the swing

**SUCCESS METRIC:** Solid contact on 7/10 shots

#### B. Mid-Range Shots (10 balls)
- Use 7 or 8-iron
- Targets 50-75 yards
- Three-quarter swings
- Focus on rhythm and balance

**SUCCESS METRIC:** Finish in balance on every shot

## STATION 1: DRIVER - DISTANCE & ACCURACY

**Purpose:** Eliminate slice, add distance, improve consistency

**Setup Needs:** Driver, alignment stick (optional), sharpie for ball marking

**Time Allocation:** 20-25 minutes

---

### BEGINNER LEVEL: Setup Fundamentals (First 3 Sessions)

**Focus:** Get setup correct BEFORE worrying about swing

#### Drill 1A: Grip Position for Straighter Drives (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 3.3

**Steps:**
1. Hold club OUTSIDE left hip
2. Let arm hang naturally
3. Place club in hand in this position
4. Hit 10 balls with this stronger grip

**Success Metric:** Ball starts more left or straight (less slice)

#### Drill 1B: Handle Position Check (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 3.3

**Steps:**
1. Set up with handle LEVEL with club head (not behind)
2. Keep hands in NEUTRAL or HIGH position
3. Check: shaft should be 90¬∞ to ground
4. Hit 10 balls focusing ONLY on maintaining this position at address

**Success Metric:** Less slice spin, ball draws or goes straight

#### Drill 1C: Forearm Alignment (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 3.3

**Steps:**
1. Set up normally
2. Check: right forearm SLIGHTLY BELOW left forearm
3. This encourages inside-out path
4. Hit 10 balls with this setup

**Success Metric:** Inside-out swing path, draw spin

**BEGINNER SESSION TOTAL:** 15 minutes, 30 balls

---

### INTERMEDIATE LEVEL: Adding Motion (After mastering setup)

**Focus:** Proper backswing rotation and sequencing

#### Drill 1D: Rib Stretch for Rotation (8 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 5.1

**Steps:**
1. Take setup with driver
2. Pick a rib on RIGHT side
3. On backswing: stretch this rib as FAR AWAY from ball as possible
4. Keep arms passive - let them swing WITH body
5. Practice swings: 5 slow-motion, 5 half-speed, 5 full-speed
6. Then hit 15 balls with this ONE thought

**Success Metric:** More rotation, creates space to swing from inside

#### Drill 1E: No Lobster Grip (8 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 1.4

**Steps:**
1. Set up normally
2. Open/release index finger and thumb of RIGHT HAND
3. Make 5 practice swings feeling club fall naturally
4. Re-grip softly (especially right hand)
5. Hit 15 balls keeping right hand SOFT

**Success Metric:** Club drops into proper "angel angle," right elbow folds down, increased lag

#### Drill 1F: Right Shoulder Behind (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 6.4

**Steps:**
1. Set up normally
2. ONE thought: Keep right shoulder BEHIND you in transition
3. Don't let right shoulder come FORWARD too early
4. Hit 10 balls with this thought

**Success Metric:** Club path moves to right, converts slice to draw

**INTERMEDIATE SESSION TOTAL:** 21 minutes, 40 balls

---

### ADVANCED LEVEL: Power Generation (After consistent contact)

**Focus:** Maximum distance with control

#### Drill 1G: Red Cross Drill (10 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 3.6

**Steps:**
1. Draw big red cross on golf ball with sharpie
2. Place on tee with cross NOT square - turn it towards you slightly, angle down
3. Hit 20 balls focusing ONLY on hitting the red cross
4. Don't think about mechanics - just hit the cross

**Success Metric:** Inside-out path, upward attack angle, simplified focus

#### Drill 1H: Glide and Push (10 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 6.2

**Steps:**
1. Feel "glide" phase: right pocket moves toward target BEFORE top of backswing
2. Then PUSH hard into ground with left foot
3. Practice sequence:
   - 5 slow-motion swings feeling glide and push
   - 5 half-speed swings
   - 10 full-power drives

**Success Metric:** More power, better sequencing, prevents early extension

#### Drill 1I: Radius Maintenance (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 3.5

**Steps:**
1. ONE thought: Keep hands same distance from body from start to strike
2. Mental cue: "Hands to upper body"
3. Hit 10 balls focusing on maintaining radius

**Success Metric:** Maximum club head speed, wider arc, more distance

**ADVANCED SESSION TOTAL:** 25 minutes, 40 balls

## STATION 2: IRON PLAY - PURE STRIKING

**Purpose:** Ball-first contact, divot after ball, consistent compression

**Setup Needs:** 6-iron and PW, alignment stick, towel, broken tee

**Time Allocation:** 20-25 minutes

---

### BEGINNER LEVEL: Low Point Control Basics

**Focus:** Understanding and controlling where club bottoms out

#### Drill 2A: Feet Together Drill (8 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.3

**Steps:**
1. Place feet together (touching)
2. **Stage 1 (5 practice swings):** No ball, feel centralized position
3. **Stage 2 (10 balls):** Hit actual shots with feet together
4. Focus: Stay centered, can't move too far or you'll fall over

**Success Metric:** Clean contact, divot starts at ball or slightly after, 7/10 pure strikes

#### Drill 2B: Forward Tee Drill (8 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.6

**Steps:**
1. Place broken tee 2 inches (4-5 cm) AHEAD of ball
2. Focus your EYES on the tee, not the ball
3. Try to hit the tee away, collecting ball on the way
4. Hit 15 balls looking at tee throughout

**Success Metric:** Ball-first contact, forward divot, reduced anxiety about hitting ball

#### Drill 2C: Eyes Ahead Practice (4 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.6

**Steps:**
1. No props needed
2. Look at grass 3-4 cm AHEAD of ball instead of at ball
3. Hit 10 balls with eyes on this forward spot

**Success Metric:** Natural forward low point, mental freedom

**BEGINNER SESSION TOTAL:** 20 minutes, 35 balls

---

### INTERMEDIATE LEVEL: Posture and Weight Transfer

**Focus:** Maintaining position and moving low point forward

#### Drill 2D: Tee to Right Pocket Drill (10 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 5.4

**Steps:**
1. Insert tee into grip end of club
2. **Takeaway:** Point tee to right pocket
3. Keep tee pointing to right pocket AS LONG AS POSSIBLE
4. At top: tee naturally points at ball
5. **Practice sequence:**
   - 5 slow-motion swings watching tee
   - 5 half-speed swings
   - 15 full swings with ball

**Success Metric:** Better body-arm coordination, prevents over-swinging, natural swing plane

#### Drill 2E: Towel Drill (8 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.6

**Steps:**
1. Place towel on ground one club head width BEHIND ball
2. Goal: Hit ball while missing towel
3. Hit 15 balls
4. If you hit towel, low point is too far back

**Success Metric:** Miss towel on every shot, center of pelvis forward at strike

#### Drill 2F: Alignment Stick Stability (7 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.5

**Steps:**
1. Place alignment stick in ground next to right leg (diagonal)
2. Right foot stays against stick during backswing
3. This makes right thigh bone axis of backswing
4. Hit 15 balls maintaining contact with stick

**Success Metric:** Prevents excessive rightward shift, easier to get forward on downswing

**INTERMEDIATE SESSION TOTAL:** 25 minutes, 45 balls

---

### ADVANCED LEVEL: Dynamic Motion and Speed

**Focus:** Power while maintaining low point control

#### Drill 2G: Stomp Drill (10 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.3

**Steps:**
1. **Start slow-motion** until you get coordination
2. As you finish backswing, lift left foot off ground
3. STOMP it back down as you start downswing
4. Progression:
   - 5 slow-motion stomps (no ball)
   - 5 half-speed stomps (no ball)
   - 15 full swings with ball and stomp

**Success Metric:** Low point moves forward, more power (lateral + vertical + rotational force)

#### Drill 2H: Skater Move (10 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 4.5

**Steps:**
1. Think: pushing off back skate on ice
2. Push under right foot to move LEFT during transition
3. Start pushing LEFT before backswing is complete
4. Progression:
   - 5 slow-motion feeling the push
   - 5 half-speed
   - 15 full swings

**Success Metric:** Center of chest and pelvis move forward, pure powerful strikes

#### Drill 2I: Game Face Integration (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 7.2

**Steps:**
1. Combine any drill above WITH:
   - Mouth slightly open
   - No tension in jaw
   - Soft shoulders, arms, hands
2. Hit 10 balls focusing on relaxation + technique

**Success Metric:** Better sequencing, more natural motion, improved feel

**ADVANCED SESSION TOTAL:** 25 minutes, 40 balls

## STATION 3: SHORT GAME - CHIPPING

**Purpose:** Consistent contact, distance control, one-piece motion

**Setup Needs:** SW and PW, head cover (optional)

**Time Allocation:** 15 minutes

---

### BEGINNER LEVEL: One Moving Part

**Focus:** Chest rotation only, arms connected

#### Drill 3A: Setup Fundamentals (3 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 8.1

**Steps:**
1. Same GOOD POSTURE as full swing
2. Same GRIP as full swing
3. Tilted forward from hips (not upright)
4. Practice position: 5 setups, checking posture each time

**Success Metric:** Comfortable, athletic position

#### Drill 3B: Chest Rotation Practice (6 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 8.1

**Steps:**
1. Arms feel "stuck to sides"
2. ONE moving part: chest/rib cage rotation
3. **Distance control:**
   - Short shot = short turn
   - Longer shot = longer turn
4. **Practice:**
   - 5 practice swings (no ball) feeling chest rotation
   - 10 short chips (10-15 feet) - short turn
   - 10 medium chips (20-30 feet) - medium turn

**Success Metric:** Where body goes, hands/arms/club follow naturally

#### Drill 3C: Avoid Windscreen Wipers (6 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 8.2

**Steps:**
1. Focus on what NOT to do:
   - Don't stand upright with rounded shoulders
   - Don't let arms move independently
   - Don't use just hands and wrists
2. Hit 10 chips focusing on:
   - Good posture maintained
   - Chest rotation
   - Arms connected to body turn

**Success Metric:** Consistent movement = consistent ball reaction

**BEGINNER SESSION TOTAL:** 15 minutes, 30 chips

---

### INTERMEDIATE LEVEL: Exaggerated Connection

**Focus:** Build the feel of one-piece motion

#### Drill 3D: Head Cover Drill (8 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 8.3

**Steps:**
1. Place head cover under BOTH arms
2. For SHORT chips only (this is restrictive)
3. Simple formula:
   - Beautiful posture
   - Beautiful grip
   - Turn back (chest)
   - Turn through (chest)
   - Collect ball on the way
4. Hit 15 really short chips with head cover

**Success Metric:** Exaggerated feeling of arms connected to body, no independent arm/hand movement

#### Drill 3E: Graduate to Normal Chips (7 min)
**Steps:**
1. Remove head cover
2. Keep the FEELING of connection
3. Now allow arms to bend and rotate more for longer shots
4. Hit 15 chips of varying distances (10-40 feet)
5. Maintain fundamental concept of body rotation

**Success Metric:** Connection maintained without restriction, natural distance variation

**INTERMEDIATE SESSION TOTAL:** 15 minutes, 30 chips

---

### ADVANCED LEVEL: Swing Geometry

**Focus:** Perfect angles, staying in posture

#### Drill 3F: Shoulder Geometry (15 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 8.4

**Steps:**
1. **Key principle:** Everything 90¬∞ to spine position
2. **Feel:**
   - Backswing: Left shoulder DOWN
   - Through swing: Right shoulder DOWN
3. **Practice:**
   - 5 slow-motion swings feeling shoulders down
   - 10 chips focusing on left shoulder down
   - 10 chips focusing on right shoulder down
   - 10 chips combining both

**Success Metric:** Stay in posture throughout, no standing up, consistent loft delivery

**ADVANCED SESSION TOTAL:** 15 minutes, 30 chips

## STATION 4: PUTTING FUNDAMENTALS

**Purpose:** Confidence, feel, consistency

**Setup Needs:** Putter, tees, alignment stick (optional)

**Time Allocation:** 15 minutes

---

### BEGINNER LEVEL: Setup and Basics

**Focus:** Grip, ball position, alignment

#### Drill 4A: Thumbs Grip Option (2 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.2 | Video 051 (p72Azozo2Aw)

**About:** Jean Jacque (biomechanist, David Leadbetter's colleague) taught this unique grip

**Steps:**
1. Fit joints of thumbs together into natural arc on putter grip
2. Hands/fingers wrap naturally around back of grip
3. Hands positioned more toward each other (not on top of grip)
4. Make 10 practice strokes feeling the thumbs-together connection

**Success Metric:** Smooth roll, ball sticks to grass as it takes off, no "jumpy" roll

**Benefits:** Enhanced left-right coordination, better rhythm, superior ball roll

#### Drill 4B: Ball Position & Eye Alignment Check (3 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.3 | Video 054 (_oS9KEdrv9M)

**Steps:**
1. Position relative to STERNUM, not feet
2. Ball underneath LEFT eye
3. **Test:** Put ball in left pocket, drop it from left eye, should land on ball on ground
4. **Eye Alignment Check:** Ensure eyes are parallel to target line
   - Eyes should NEVER be beyond the ball (outside target line)
   - Acceptable: Eyes over ball or slightly inside
   - **TURN your head** to look at hole (correct)
   - **Don't TILT your head** to look at hole (causes eyes to go offline)
5. Practice this position for 5 putts

**Success Metric:** No backspin, clean roll without jumping, ball starts on intended line

**Key:** All three must align: club head, body, AND eyes. Club head swings where eyes point!

#### Drill 4C: Confidence Builder with Tee (6 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.1

**Steps:**
1. Place tee in ground as target
2. **Progression:**
   - 5 putts from 6 inches (make all 5)
   - 5 putts from 1 foot (make all 5)
   - 5 putts from 2 feet (make 4/5)
   - 5 putts from 3 feet (make 3/5)

**Success Metric:** Build confidence with short putts, hitting tee builds confidence for hitting hole

#### Drill 4D: Aim the Face (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.4

**Steps:**
1. Use alignment stick or edge of mat
2. Set up with putter face 90¬∞ to target line
3. Make 10 putts from 6 feet
4. Focus ONLY on face alignment (not path yet)

**Success Metric:** Even with imperfect stroke, proper face aim sends ball on target

**BEGINNER SESSION TOTAL:** 16 minutes, 35 putts

---

### INTERMEDIATE LEVEL: Routine and Visualization

**Focus:** Pre-shot routine, mental game

#### Drill 4E: Eye Trigger (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.5

**Steps:**
1. Setup: Take time for perfect alignment
2. Look at hole and visualize where ball will fall over edge
3. **Trigger:** As soon as eyes return to ball, IMMEDIATELY start stroke
4. **Formula:** Hole ‚Üí Ball ‚Üí GO (no delay)
5. Practice 10 putts from various distances (3-15 feet)

**Success Metric:** No freezing, consistent rhythm, no delay in execution

#### Drill 4F: Visualization Practice (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.6

**Steps:**
1. For each putt, visualize success 3 times:
   - Once while reading putt
   - Twice during address (two looks at hole)
2. Minimize time between last look and execution
3. Practice 8 putts from 6-12 feet

**Success Metric:** Clear mental picture, confident stroke

#### Drill 4G: No Practice Swing (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.6

**Steps:**
1. Trust instinctive distance control
2. Make last look at hole the most important one
3. Look at hole, then immediately react
4. Practice 8 putts from varying distances (10-30 feet)
5. Focus on FEEL, not mechanics

**Success Metric:** Natural feel for distance, reduced mechanical thoughts

#### Drill 4H: Around the Clock - Andrew's #1 Putting Drill (15 min)
**Reference:** Video 055 (q7Swfq0luq4) - Putting Tip #10

**Andrew's Note:** "If you ignore all other 99 tips, don't ignore this one. This drill has changed people's handicaps."

**Setup:**
1. Place 4 balls at 1 meter (3 feet) from the hole:
   - Position 12:00 - Straight uphill (confidence builder)
   - Position 3:00 - Right to left breaking
   - Position 6:00 - Straight downhill
   - Position 9:00 - Left to right breaking

**Training Aid (Optional):**
- Lay flagstick on ground to check:
  - Club face square at address
  - Swing path (inside-square-inside)
  - Consistency of stroke

**The Challenge:**
1. Go around the clock 5 complete times (20 total putts)
2. Use your SAME routine on every putt (just like on course)
3. **Goal:** Make 17 out of 20 putts before finishing practice
4. Don't leave until you hit the standard!

**Success Metric:** 17/20 makes or better, consistent routine on every putt

**Benefits:**
- Practices all types of putts (straight, breaking, uphill, downhill)
- Builds confidence from multiple angles
- Creates measurable standard for improvement
- Simulates on-course pressure

**INTERMEDIATE SESSION TOTAL:** 30 minutes, 54 putts

### ADVANCED LEVEL: Pressure and Special Shots

**Focus:** Challenging situations, competition prep

#### Drill 4H: Dennis Wise - Five Footers (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.7

**Steps:**
1. Find straight 5-foot putt
2. Place tee at edge of hole where you want ball to roll over
3. Focus on rolling ball over PINK TEE (not hole)
4. Look at tee when looking up
5. Keep mental picture of tee during stroke
6. Hit 10 five-footers

**Success Metric:** Make 7/10, more precise focus zone

#### Drill 4I: Downhill Putts - Toe Strike (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.7

**Steps:**
1. Find downhill putt
2. Address ball out of TOE of putter (not sweet spot)
3. Make normal positive stroke
4. Still look at front edge of ball
5. Practice 8 downhill putts

**Success Metric:** Better speed control on fast putts, reduced energy transfer

#### Drill 4J: Forget Lag - Always Hole It (5 min)
**Reference:** Master Summary Section 9.6

**Steps:**
1. Find long putts (20-40 feet)
2. ALWAYS visualize ball going in hole (not getting close)
3. Visualize ball "dying over edge" at specific entry point
4. Focus on FEEL at strike to get distance right
5. Practice 8 long putts

**Success Metric:** Better instinctive distance control, more aggressive mindset

#### Drill 4K: Fringe Putting Visual Sequence (Phil Mickelson Tip) (5 min)
**Reference:** Video 052 (KVlbzMQgOqo)

**Key Principle:** Worst putt is always better than worst chip - always putt from fringe!

**Steps:**
1. Find a putt with ball on fringe, hole on green
2. Set up with normal putting routine
3. **Visual Sequence:** Fringe edge ‚Üí Hole ‚Üí Ball ‚Üí GO
4. Repeat: Look where fringe meets green, then hole, then ball
5. This feeds your subconscious the critical information about resistance change
6. Practice 8 fringe putts of varying distances

**Success Metric:** Better pace control from fringe, ball reaches hole consistently

**Why It Works:** Accounts for extra resistance from fringe grass vs green grass, helps calibrate proper pace automatically

**ADVANCED SESSION TOTAL:** 20 minutes, 34 putts

## WEEKLY PRACTICE SCHEDULE

### BEGINNER PROGRAM (First 4-8 Weeks)

**Goal:** Build fundamentals, establish feel, develop consistency

**Week 1-2: Foundation**
- **Day 1 (60 min):** Warm-up + Driver Setup (Beginner) + Putting Basics
- **Day 2 (60 min):** Warm-up + Iron Low Point (Beginner) + Chipping Basics
- **Day 3 (30 min):** Warm-up + Short Game Focus

**Week 3-4: Repetition**
- **Day 1 (60 min):** Warm-up + Driver Setup (Beginner) + Iron Low Point (Beginner)
- **Day 2 (60 min):** Warm-up + Chipping Basics + Putting Basics
- **Day 3 (60 min):** Warm-up + Iron Low Point (Beginner) + Driver Setup (Beginner)

**Week 5-6: Testing**
- **Day 1 (90 min):** Warm-up + Driver (Beginner) + Iron (Beginner) + Short Game
- **Day 2 (60 min):** Warm-up + Weakest Area + Putting
- **Day 3 (60 min):** Warm-up + Full Bag Test (hit every club once)

**Week 7-8: Consolidation**
- **Day 1 (90 min):** Full intensive session (Beginner level all stations)
- **Day 2 (60 min):** Problem areas + Putting
- **Day 3 (30-60 min):** Short game focus

**Progress Check:** Can you hit 7/10 solid shots with driver and irons? Move to Intermediate level.

---

### INTERMEDIATE PROGRAM (Weeks 8-20)

**Goal:** Add motion, improve consistency, increase power

**Typical Week:**
- **Day 1 (90 min):** Warm-up + Driver (Intermediate) + Iron (Intermediate) + Putting
- **Day 2 (60 min):** Warm-up + Short Game (Intermediate) + Putting (Intermediate)
- **Day 3 (60 min):** Warm-up + Problem area focus + Video analysis

**Monthly Focus:**
- **Month 1:** Body rotation and sequencing
- **Month 2:** Low point control and weight transfer
- **Month 3:** Integration and consistency

**Progress Check:** Consistent ball-first contact? Understanding sequencing? Move to Advanced level.

---

### ADVANCED PROGRAM (Week 20+)

**Goal:** Power generation, course preparation, competition readiness

**Typical Week:**
- **Day 1 (90 min):** Warm-up + Driver (Advanced) + Iron (Advanced) + Short Game
- **Day 2 (60 min):** Warm-up + Putting (Advanced) + Pressure drills
- **Day 3 (90 min):** Warm-up + Course simulation (play imaginary holes)

**Monthly Themes:**
- Rotate focus: Distance month, Accuracy month, Short game month, All-around month

**Maintenance:** Keep doing fundamentals drills periodically to maintain base

### Success Metrics by Station

#### DRIVER SUCCESS METRICS:
- **Beginner:** 7/10 balls start on target line or left (for slicers)
- **Intermediate:** 7/10 fairways hit, consistent ball flight
- **Advanced:** 8/10 fairways hit, average 10+ yards gained

#### IRON SUCCESS METRICS:
- **Beginner:** 7/10 ball-first contact, divot after ball
- **Intermediate:** 8/10 pure strikes, consistent divot pattern
- **Advanced:** 9/10 pure strikes, consistent distance gaps

#### SHORT GAME SUCCESS METRICS:
- **Beginner:** Consistent contact, general distance control
- **Intermediate:** 7/10 chips within 6 feet of target
- **Advanced:** 8/10 chips within 3 feet of target

#### PUTTING SUCCESS METRICS:
- **Beginner:** Make 8/10 from 3 feet, 3-putt less than 2/round
- **Intermediate:** Make 6/10 from 6 feet, read breaks correctly
- **Advanced:** Make 4/10 from 10 feet, confident from all distances

## TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

### "I'm Not Seeing Improvement"

**Check:**
1. Are you doing the warm-up? (It's not optional!)
2. Are you trying to do too much at once? (Pick ONE station)
3. Are you doing drills in order? (Beginner ‚Üí Intermediate ‚Üí Advanced)
4. Are you tracking what works? (Use the notes template)
5. Are you practicing the right level? (Be honest about current skill)

**Solution:** Go back to beginner level and rebuild fundamentals

---

### "I Don't Have Time for Full Sessions"

**Mini-Sessions That Work:**
- **15 min:** Warm-up (body wake-up only) + 10 putts
- **20 min:** Warm-up + One drill from your weakest station
- **25 min:** Warm-up + 10 chips + 10 putts (short game intensive)

**Remember:** 3 focused 30-minute sessions beat 1 unfocused 90-minute session

---

### "I'm Confused About Which Drill to Do"

**Simple Decision Tree:**

**Is your miss a slice?** ‚Üí Driver Station (Setup Fundamentals)

**Are you hitting fat/thin?** ‚Üí Iron Station (Low Point Control)

**Is chipping inconsistent?** ‚Üí Short Game Station (One Moving Part)

**Are you 3-putting?** ‚Üí Putting Station (Setup and Basics)

**Everything is inconsistent?** ‚Üí Start with Warm-up Routine only for 2 weeks

---

### "The Range Doesn't Have X Equipment"

**Substitutions:**
- **No alignment stick?** Use your club on ground or a piece of string
- **No broken tee?** Use a coin or leaf
- **No towel?** Draw a line in dirt/sand
- **No head cover?** Use glove or rolled-up jacket
- **No impact tape?** Use baby powder or foot spray

**Remember Andrew's philosophy:** "I'm not a lover of training aids. Just have to be a little creative."
