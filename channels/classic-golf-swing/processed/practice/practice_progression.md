# Ben Hogan Practice Progression: 8-Week Program

This week-by-week program follows Hogan's sequential building block approach. **Do not skip weeks.** Each week builds on the previous.

---

## Week 0: Assessment & Preparation

### Goals
- Understand the system
- Establish baseline
- Prepare for change

### Tasks
- [ ] Read all 5 video summaries
- [ ] Read master_summary.md completely
- [ ] Film your current swing (DTL and face-on)
- [ ] Note current ball flight patterns
- [ ] Gather tools: mirror, alignment sticks, impact spray
- [ ] Commit to the 8-week process

### Mindset
Accept that you'll be rebuilding from the ground up. Some things will feel worse before they feel better. Trust the process.

---

## Week 1-2: Lesson 1 - The Grip

### Primary Focus
Master Hogan's two-grip system: lead hand in palm, trail hand in fingers

### Daily Practice (30 minutes)

#### Day 1: Lead Hand Only
- 10 min: Place club in crook of first finger 50 times
- 10 min: Check palm placement and wrist arch in mirror
- 10 min: Hold club with lead hand only, make slow practice swings

#### Day 2: Trail Hand Only
- 10 min: Focus on middle two "Bruce Lee fingers" placement
- 10 min: Feel pressure point under knuckle
- 10 min: Awareness of swing wreckers staying passive

#### Day 3: Combined Grip
- 15 min: Put both hands together 30 times
- 15 min: Practice swings focusing on grip only (no ball)

#### Day 4: Mirror Work
- 20 min: Set grip 20 times checking all positions in mirror
- 10 min: Make swings monitoring grip throughout

#### Day 5: Feel Development
- 10 min: Close eyes, set grip by feel only, open eyes to verify
- 20 min: Practice swings building muscle memory

#### Day 6-7: Integration & Review
- 30 min: Combine all grip elements, add balls if grip feels consistent
- Focus: Can you set identical grip without thinking?

### Key Checkpoints
- [ ] Lead hand: Club in crook of first finger
- [ ] Lead hand: Runs across palm (not fingers)
- [ ] Lead hand: Slight wrist arch visible
- [ ] Trail hand: Middle two fingers do the work
- [ ] Trail hand: Outer fingers passive (swing wreckers)
- [ ] Can set grip identically 10 times in a row

### Success Criteria
✓ Grip feels natural (not forced)
✓ Can set it consistently without mirror
✓ Swing wreckers stay passive through full swing
✓ Ready to add stance elements

**If not ready**: Repeat week 2 until grip is automatic

---

## Week 3-4: Lesson 2 - The Stance

### Primary Focus
Master foot position, weight distribution, and body connection

### Daily Practice (35 minutes)

#### Day 1: Foot Angles
- 15 min: Use alignment stick to check trail foot 90° (perpendicular)
- 10 min: Practice lead foot open 1/4 turn
- 10 min: Step into stance 20 times getting angles right

#### Day 2: Weight Distribution
- 15 min: Weight in heels drill (lift toes slightly to feel)
- 10 min: Make swings focusing on keeping weight in heels
- 10 min: Notice difference from toe-weight setup

#### Day 3: Connection
- 15 min: Towel/glove under armpits drill
- 10 min: Feel inside leg muscles engaged
- 10 min: Practice swings maintaining connection

#### Day 4: Integration
- 10 min: Set grip (from weeks 1-2)
- 15 min: Add stance elements one at a time
- 10 min: Full setup routine: grip → stance → check

#### Day 5: Half Swings
- 10 min: Setup routine (grip + stance)
- 25 min: Half swings focusing on maintaining setup positions
- Balls optional - focus is on feeling, not results

#### Day 6-7: Full Integration
- 35 min: Complete setup (grip + stance), 3/4 to full swings
- Monitor: Weight stays in heels throughout?
- Focus: Does stance feel athletic and balanced?

### Key Checkpoints
- [ ] Trail foot: 90° to target line (use stick to verify)
- [ ] Lead foot: Open 1/4 turn
- [ ] Weight: Feel it deep in heels
- [ ] Arms: Connected to chest (towel doesn't drop)
- [ ] Grip: Still automatic from weeks 1-2
- [ ] Balance: Stable and centered at address

### Success Criteria
✓ Can set stance quickly without thinking
✓ Weight naturally stays in heels through swing
✓ Foot angles feel natural
✓ Grip still consistent (hasn't regressed)
✓ Ready to add backswing sequence

**If not ready**: Repeat week 4 focusing on weight in heels

---

## Week 5-6: Lesson 3 - The Backswing

### Primary Focus
Master sequence (hands→arms→shoulders→hips), brace rear leg, stay under plane

### Daily Practice (40 minutes)

#### Day 1: Rear Leg Brace
- 10 min: Setup (grip + stance from previous weeks)
- 20 min: Half swings feeling rear leg as stable post
- 10 min: Monitor - does leg stay braced or collapse?

#### Day 2: The Sequence
- 10 min: Setup review
- 20 min: Ultra-slow backswings with pauses:
  - Hands start → pause → Arms follow → pause → Shoulders → pause → Hips
- 10 min: Gradually speed up maintaining sequence

#### Day 3: Plane Work
- 10 min: Setup
- 20 min: "Lead hand to opposite hip pocket" drill
- 10 min: Video check - is club staying under plane?

#### Day 4: Hip Opening
- 10 min: Setup
- 20 min: Allow hips to open, lead knee breaks IN
- 10 min: Feel foot roll to instep, natural heel lift

#### Day 5: Integration
- 10 min: Complete setup (grip + stance)
- 30 min: Full backswings combining all elements:
  - Brace rear leg
  - Sequence: hands → arms → shoulders → hips
  - Stay under plane (hip pocket feel)
  - Allow hips to open

#### Day 6-7: Ball Striking
- 40 min: Full setup and backswing, add balls
- ONE THOUGHT per swing (rotate through):
  - "Rear leg braced"
  - "Lead hand to hip pocket"
  - "Let hips open"
- Focus on feel, not ball flight

### Key Checkpoints
- [ ] Rear leg: Braced throughout (stable post)
- [ ] Sequence: Hands → Arms → Shoulders → Hips (visible on video)
- [ ] Plane: Club stays under "pane of glass"
- [ ] Lead hand: Feels like reaching to opposite hip pocket
- [ ] Hips: Allowed to turn/open
- [ ] Lead knee: Breaks IN toward rear leg
- [ ] Balanced at top of backswing

### Success Criteria
✓ Rear leg stays braced automatically
✓ Sequence feels smooth (not jerky)
✓ Club stays on plane (verified by video)
✓ Balanced position at top
✓ Grip and stance still automatic
✓ Ready to add downswing

**If not ready**: Extra week on plane work (most common need)

---

## Week 7-8: Lesson 4 - The Downswing

### Primary Focus
Hips initiate, lateral weight shift first, upper body pulled through

### Daily Practice (45 minutes)

#### Day 1: Belt Grab Drill
- 10 min: Setup + backswing (all previous lessons)
- 25 min: Belt grab drill (20-30 reps)
  - Lead hand grabs belt at hip
  - Backswing with trail hand
  - Feel hip pull back from top
  - Experience hips leading
- 10 min: Apply feeling to practice swings

#### Day 2: Weight Transfer Awareness
- 10 min: Complete setup + backswing
- 25 min: Feet-together drill (exaggerates shift)
  - Start with feet together
  - Step toward target with lead foot as you swing
  - Feel weight transfer
- 10 min: Apply to normal stance

#### Day 3: The Sequence
- 10 min: Setup + backswing
- 25 min: Pause drill at transition
  - Top of backswing → pause
  - Feel hips shift laterally → pause
  - Feel hips begin to rotate → pause
  - Complete swing
- 10 min: Gradually eliminate pauses

#### Day 4: Elastic Band Feel
- 10 min: Setup + backswing
- 25 min: Visualize elastic on lead hip
  - Feel it "snap back" from top
  - Hips pull everything through
- 10 min: Practice without visualization (feel should be ingrained)

#### Day 5: Balanced Finish
- 10 min: Complete setup + backswing
- 35 min: Full swings with finish hold
  - MUST hold finish for 3 seconds
  - If can't hold = downswing sequence was wrong
  - Work backward to identify issue

#### Day 6-7: Full Integration
- 45 min: Complete swing (all 4 lessons integrated)
- Add balls for majority of session
- ONE thought per swing:
  - "Hips first"
  - "Elastic snaps"
  - "Weight shifts"
  - "Balanced finish"
- Film swing - check all positions

### Key Checkpoints
- [ ] Transition: Hips move first (not hands!)
- [ ] Weight: Shifts laterally before rotating
- [ ] Hips: Clear/rotate creating space
- [ ] Upper body: Pulled through (passive)
- [ ] Impact: Weight predominantly on lead side
- [ ] Finish: Balanced (can hold 3+ seconds)
- [ ] All previous lessons: Still automatic

### Success Criteria
✓ Hips initiate instinctively (hands don't start)
✓ Weight transfers smoothly
✓ Can hold balanced finish on 8/10 swings
✓ Grip, stance, backswing still feel natural
✓ Contact quality improved (more solid)
✓ Ready for integration phase

**If not ready**: Extra week on hips-first sequence (most critical element)

## Week 9+: Integration & Mastery

### Goals
- All 4 fundamentals operating automatically
- One-thought-at-a-time practice
- Gradual return to on-course play

### Weekly Practice Structure (Flexible)

#### Practice Session 1: Fundamental Review (45 min)
- 5 min: Grip check (still correct after weeks of practice?)
- 5 min: Stance check (foot angles, weight in heels)
- 10 min: Backswing focus (brace, plane, sequence)
- 15 min: Downswing focus (hips first, weight shift, balance)
- 10 min: Full integration hitting balls

#### Practice Session 2: One-Thought Rounds (60 min)
- Warm-up: 10 min reviewing all 4 fundamentals
- Round 1 (10 min): Every swing = "Middle two fingers"
- Round 2 (10 min): Every swing = "Weight in heels"
- Round 3 (10 min): Every swing = "Rear leg brace"
- Round 4 (10 min): Every swing = "Lead hand to hip pocket"
- Round 5 (10 min): Every swing = "Hips first"
- Cool down: Free swinging, trust the fundamentals

#### Practice Session 3: Performance Practice (60 min)
- 10 min: Warm-up
- 40 min: Simulated on-course situations
  - Pick specific targets
  - Go through pre-shot routine (quick fundamental check)
  - Hit shot with ONE swing thought
  - Move to next "hole"
- 10 min: Balanced finish check - film last 5 swings

#### Practice Session 4: Video Review (30 min)
- Film 5-10 swings
- Check each fundamental:
  - Grip: Still correct?
  - Stance: Foot angles, weight position?
  - Backswing: Sequence visible? Club on plane?
  - Downswing: Hips lead? Weight transfers? Balanced finish?
- Note: Which fundamental needs attention next session?

### Monthly Check-In

Every 4 weeks, assess:

**Grip:**
- [ ] Still setting it correctly automatically?
- [ ] Swing wreckers staying passive?
- [ ] Feels natural?

**Stance:**
- [ ] Foot angles automatic?
- [ ] Weight still in heels throughout swing?
- [ ] Connection maintained?

**Backswing:**
- [ ] Rear leg stays braced?
- [ ] Sequence smooth (hands→arms→shoulders→hips)?
- [ ] Club on plane (verified by video)?

**Downswing:**
- [ ] Hips initiate instinctively?
- [ ] Weight transfers smoothly?
- [ ] Balanced finish on most swings?

**If any regressed**: Spend a week re-focusing on that element

### Rotating Focus System

**Week 1 of month**: Grip + Stance emphasis
**Week 2 of month**: Backswing emphasis
**Week 3 of month**: Downswing emphasis
**Week 4 of month**: Full integration, minimal swing thoughts

### On-Course Integration

**First 3 Months:**
- Play front 9 or executive courses
- Focus on fundamentals, not score
- One swing thought per hole
- Forgive bad shots (swing still developing)

**Months 4-6:**
- Gradually return to regular courses
- Still keep ONE swing thought per round
- Trust fundamentals more, think less
- Celebrate improved ball striking, not just scores

**Months 6+:**
- Fundamentals becoming truly automatic
- Minimal swing thoughts needed
- Focus shifts to course management and strategy
- Periodic video checks to ensure no regression

### Long-Term Maintenance

**Weekly:**
- At least one practice session reviewing all 4 fundamentals
- Film swing monthly to catch any regression early

**Monthly:**
- Re-read quick_reference.md
- Watch one of the 5 video summaries as a refresher

**Quarterly:**
- Re-read master_summary.md
- Full video analysis of all 4 fundamentals
- Note improvements and areas still developing

## Troubleshooting

### "I'm stuck on Week X and not progressing"

**Most Common Cause**: Trying to rush. The fundamental doesn't feel natural yet.

**Solution**:
- Spend another week on that lesson
- Slow down practice (more feel work, less ball hitting)
- Video yourself to see if you're actually doing what you think you're doing
- Some golfers need 3-4 weeks per lesson - that's fine!

### "I mastered Lesson 2, but now Lesson 1 (grip) feels off"

**Cause**: Common regression when adding new elements.

**Solution**:
- Start each practice session with 5-min grip review
- Fundamentals need ongoing maintenance, not just one-time learning
- When learning new lesson, always warm up with previous lessons

### "My ball striking got WORSE"

**Cause**: Expected! You're rebuilding fundamental movements.

**Solution**:
- This is NORMAL in weeks 1-6
- Trust the process - Hogan's fundamentals work
- Focus on positions and feel, not ball flight
- Improvement in ball striking typically comes in weeks 7-12
- Some golfers see it sooner, some later - be patient

### "I'm overwhelmed with all the checkpoints"

**Cause**: Trying to think about too many things at once.

**Solution**:
- During practice: Focus on ONE element per session
- When hitting balls: ONE thought per swing
- Use rotating focus - grip today, stance tomorrow, etc.
- The checkpoints are for setup and review, not for during the swing
