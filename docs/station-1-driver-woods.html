Station 1: Driver & Woods

Develop consistent contact and shot shaping with driver and fairway woods

Takeaway Drills

Purpose

Train the synchronized movement of shoulders, trunk, arms, and wrists moving together as one block rather than using arms alone.

Setup

  • Grip the club lower on the shaft
  • Position at address as normal

Execution

  • Rotate the torso to move all components together
  • The club butt should remain pointed at your belly during the takeaway
  • Wrist hinging should begin only when hands reach hip height

Goal

Avoid initiating wrist action too early in the takeaway. Achieve synchronized, one-piece movement.

Purpose

Learn to bring the club straight back rather than too far inside or outside the target line.

Setup

  • Use a practice basket or bucket
  • Hold it with hands on each side as you would a golf club
  • Position at address as normal

Execution

  • Bring the basket back to hip height
  • Check that the opening points directly away from the target
  • This indicates a square takeaway path

Goal

Prevent rolling hands inward (inside path) or pushing club outward (outside path/over-the-top error).

Backswing Drills

Purpose

Evaluate whether the club travels back along the correct swing plane rather than too steep or too flat.

Setup

  • Stand with your back near a wall
  • Make your backswing motion

Checkpoint

  • Note where the clubhead would contact the wall at the top
  • Proper on-plane: Clubhead strikes around head height
  • Too flat: Below head height
  • Too steep: Above head height

Goal

Identify swing plane issues that affect consistency and ball flight.

Purpose

Verify correct hand and club positioning at the halfway-back checkpoint.

Setup

  • Separate hands on the shaft
  • Take club back to halfway position

Checkpoint at Chest Height

  • Stop when lower hand reaches chest height
  • Grip end should point toward the ball
  • Hands should be behind the target-facing body plane
  • Not excessively inside or outside

Goal

Achieve a square backswing path that sets up proper positioning at the top.

Top of Swing Drills

Purpose

Test rotational mechanics and shoulder turn quality.

Setup

  • Hold a club across shoulders parallel to ground
  • Make backswing motion to top position

Key Checkpoints

  • Club should point toward the ball
  • Knees remain flexed (not collapsed)
  • Verify efficient upper body coiling
  • Maintain knee stability throughout

Goal

Develop proper shoulder rotation while maintaining lower body stability.

Purpose

Assess whether swing plane is on-target.

Execution

  • Reach top of swing position
  • Release wrists and let club shaft drop

Position Analysis

  • On-plane: Shaft touches shoulder bone
  • Too flat: Shaft touches upper arm
  • Too upright: Shaft touches neck

Goal

Confirm on-plane positioning for consistent ball contact.

Purpose

Verify club shaft alignment relative to target line.

Checkpoint at Top

  • Square (ideal): Shaft points at target
  • Laid off: Shaft points left of target
  • Across the line: Shaft points right of target

Goal

Proper shaft direction indicates correct hand position and backswing mechanics.

Purpose

Evaluate right hand setup for downswing initiation.

Key Checkpoints

  • Elbow bent beyond 90 degrees
  • Wrist bent at approximately 90 degrees
  • Hand pointing skyward
  • Elbow positioned below shoulder level

Goal

Establish strong position for impact sequence and power generation.

Quick Fixes - Having Trouble?

Cause

Ball starts left and curves right due to outside-in club path with clubface open relative to path. Common causes include open stance, weak grip, and over-the-top move.

Quick Fix

  • Stance: Check feet are parallel to target line (square stance, not open)
  • Grip: Strengthen grip - rotate hands so you see 2-3 knuckles on left hand
  • Path: Take club back square, avoid pushing hands away from body at top
  • Release: Allow natural rotation of hands through impact

Recommended Drill

Practice Square Takeaway Drill (Drill 2) to fix club path issues at the root.

Cause

Ball starts right and curves aggressively left due to inside-out path with clubface closed relative to path. Often caused by closed stance, strong grip, or excessive inside takeaway.

Quick Fix

  • Stance: Adjust to square stance (feet parallel to target, not closed/right)
  • Grip: Weaken grip by rotating hands to show fewer knuckles
  • Takeaway: Ensure square takeaway, not too far inside
  • Top Position: Keep left wrist flat, not bowed forward
  • Release: Control hand rotation through impact

Recommended Drill

Work on Club Direction at Top (Drill 7) to ensure shaft points at target, not laid off behind you.

Cause

Club hits ball above equator when swing arc bottom is too high, too early (before ball), or too late (after ball). Often caused by swaying, reverse pivot, standing up at impact, or poor ball position.

Quick Fix

  • Avoid Swaying: Keep right knee leaned in and flexed throughout backswing
  • Weight Transfer: Shift weight to back foot in backswing, front foot in downswing (not reverse)
  • Ball Position: Start with ball in center of stance, adjust from there
  • Setup: Distribute weight equally, let right shoulder sit naturally lower
  • Head Position: Keep head at same height from address through impact

Recommended Drill

Practice Upper Body Rotation Check (Drill 5) to maintain posture and avoid swaying or standing up.

Cause

Leading edge strikes ball below equator when swing arc bottom is too high or positioned after ball. Common causes include standing up at impact, swaying, scooping/flipping, or ball positioned too far forward.

Quick Fix

  • Keep Head in Box: Maintain same head height from address through impact
  • No Swaying: Keep right knee leaned in and flexed
  • No Scooping: Keep wrists from releasing angle through impact
  • Ball Position: Check ball isn't too far forward in stance
  • Weight Distribution: Equal weight on both feet at address
  • Spine Angle: Maintain consistent forward tilt throughout swing

Recommended Drill

Use One-Piece Takeaway Drill (Drill 1) to maintain connection and prevent early wrist release.

Cause

Distance loss often results from poor contact (fat/thin shots), insufficient swing speed, early release (casting), incomplete shoulder turn, or incorrect club selection/setup.

Quick Fix

  • Contact First: Focus on solid center-face strikes before swinging harder
  • Full Turn: Complete shoulder rotation at top of backswing
  • Maintain Lag: Keep wrist hinge through transition, release at bottom
  • Tempo: Smooth tempo often produces more distance than rushing
  • Equipment: Ensure proper tee height and ball position for driver

Recommended Drill

Work on Right Hand Position Check (Drill 8) to maintain lag and create proper angle for power release.
