Station 4: Chipping

Chip it close every time

Session Goal

Develop consistent contact and distance control around the green

Core Chipping Drills

Purpose

Train your hands to remain quiet during chip shots by focusing on shoulder rotation rather than wrist movement.

Setup

  • Narrow stance with weight forward (60/40 on front foot)
  • Hands ahead of the ball
  • Position ball center to slightly back in stance
  • Club shaft should remain clear in front of your body

Execution

  • Focus on rotating your shoulders, not breaking your wrists
  • Keep your hands quiet throughout the motion
  • The club should never strike your side during the swing
  • If the shaft hits your side, you're breaking your wrists

Goal

Develop consistent contact through body rotation instead of hand manipulation. This builds reliability and eliminates chunking and blading.

Purpose

Build confidence in chipping through complete up-and-down practice. Experience real scoring situations.

Setup

  • Place three balls at various distances around the green
  • Have your putter ready nearby
  • Choose different lies and angles

Execution

  1. Chip the first ball close to the flag
  2. Immediately putt it out
  3. Repeat with the second ball
  4. Complete all three balls in sequence
  5. Experience how much easier putts become within gimme range

Goal

Complete 10 successful up-and-downs in a practice session. This builds confidence and simulates real on-course pressure.

Purpose

Develop consistency from varying distances and lies. Build precision targeting under different conditions.

Setup

  • Create a 3-foot circle around the hole (use tees or alignment sticks)
  • Practice from various distances: 10 feet, 20 feet, 30 feet
  • Change lies between attempts (fairway, first cut, fringe)

Execution

  • Pick your landing spot on the green
  • Visualize the ball rolling to the hole
  • Execute the chip with proper technique
  • Each chip should finish within the 3-foot circle

Goal

Leave every chip within 3 feet - gimme range. Hit 10 consecutive chips into the target zone before moving on.

Purpose

Learn the air-to-roll ratios of different clubs for proper shot selection. Master club choice based on required trajectory.

Club Ratios to Learn

7-iron: 20% air / 80% roll

Use when you have lots of green to work with

Pitching Wedge: 50% air / 50% roll

The versatile middle option for most situations

Lob Wedge: 80% air / 20% roll

When you need to fly it most of the way

Execution

  1. Pick a target 30 feet from the fringe
  2. Chip with all three clubs using the same technique
  3. Note how far each ball carries vs. rolls
  4. Learn which club suits different green conditions
  5. Practice selecting the right club before each chip

Goal

Understand carry-to-roll relationships so you can instinctively choose the right club for any situation around the green.

Purpose

Master Phil Mickelson's reliable chipping technique. Create consistent strike with spin control.

Setup

  • Narrow stance, weight forward
  • Open clubface slightly for added loft
  • Hands ahead of ball at address
  • Ball positioned center of stance

Execution - The Key Move

"Hinge on the way back and up, hold on the way back down"

  • Backswing: Hinge your wrists naturally as you take the club back
  • Through Impact: HOLD that hinged position - don't release it early
  • Follow Through: Let the hinge release after impact
  • This prevents deceleration and mistiming

Common Error Prevented

Most golfers release the hinge too early, causing thin or chunked chips. Holding the hinge creates crisp, consistent contact.

Goal

Hit 20 chips in a row with crisp contact and proper trajectory. You should hear and feel solid compression of the ball.

Practice from Different Lies

Master these common situations around the green:

Characteristics

Ball sitting on short grass or hardpan with little margin for error underneath.

Setup Adjustments

  • Ball position: slightly back of center
  • Weight heavily forward (70/30)
  • Hands well ahead of the ball
  • Club selection: consider less lofted club (PW or 9-iron)

Swing Keys

  • Keep hands ahead through impact
  • Focus on brushing the grass, not digging
  • Shallow angle of attack
  • Trust the loft - don't try to scoop

Common Mistake

Trying to help the ball up leads to bladed shots. Trust your setup and make a descending blow.

Characteristics

Ball sitting up nicely in light rough or first cut. Grass underneath provides cushion.

Setup Adjustments

  • Ball position: center of stance
  • Normal weight distribution (60/40 forward)
  • Club selection: use more loft (SW or LW)
  • Expect less spin from grass between club and ball

Swing Keys

  • Slightly steeper angle of attack
  • Make sure to contact ball before grass
  • Accelerate through impact (grass will slow club)
  • Expect ball to come out softer with less spin

Distance Control

Ball typically flies 10-15% shorter than from fairway. Plan for extra roll.

Characteristics

Ball nestled down in thick grass. Limited clubface-to-ball contact possible.

Setup Adjustments

  • Open clubface more than usual
  • Narrow stance, significant forward weight
  • Ball position: center to slightly forward
  • Grip down for more control

Swing Keys

  • Steep angle of attack to minimize grass contact
  • Firm grip pressure to prevent twisting
  • Commit to aggressive acceleration
  • Accept that you can't spin it from here

Strategy

Goal is to advance the ball safely onto the green. Don't try to be a hero - take your medicine and get it on the putting surface.

Characteristics

Ball on extremely firm, bare ground. Club bounces easily, making clean contact difficult.

Setup Adjustments

  • Use less lofted club (8-iron or 9-iron)
  • Ball position: well back in stance
  • Weight significantly forward (75/25)
  • Hands way ahead of ball
  • Stand closer to ball for steeper approach

Swing Keys

  • Think "putting stroke with loft"
  • Very shallow, sweeping motion
  • Keep hands ahead throughout
  • Bump and run approach - plan for lots of roll

What to Avoid

Never use a high-lofted wedge from hardpan unless you're extremely skilled. The bounce will cause bladed shots. Safer play is bump-and-run.

Characteristics

Ball just off the green in slightly longer grass. Often the safest play is to putt.

When to Chip vs. Putt

Putt when:

  • Fringe is thin and uniform
  • No severe slopes between ball and hole
  • You're uncomfortable chipping
  • Under pressure (putting is more consistent)

Chip when:

  • Fringe is thick or uneven
  • Long distance to hole
  • Severe uphill slope ahead

Putting from Fringe Technique

  • Use your normal putting stroke
  • Hit it 20-30% firmer than normal
  • Contact ball slightly above center
  • Follow through fully

Chipping from Fringe

  • Ball position: center of stance
  • Use least lofted club needed (8i, 9i, PW)
  • Standard chipping technique
  • Land ball on green, not in fringe

Characteristics

Ball above your feet or on an upslope. Slope adds loft and makes ball fly higher and shorter.

Setup Adjustments

  • Use less lofted club (one club less than normal)
  • Align shoulders with the slope
  • More weight on downhill (back) foot to maintain balance
  • Ball position: slightly forward

Swing Keys

  • Swing along the slope, not into it
  • Accept that ball will launch higher
  • Plan for less roll due to higher trajectory
  • Keep your balance throughout

Distance Adjustment

Uphill lies add effective loft, so the ball flies higher but shorter. Expect 10-20% less distance than normal.

Quick Fixes

Having trouble? Here are instant solutions:

The Problem

Club strikes the ground before the ball, resulting in shots that travel very short distances.

Most Common Causes

  • Ball positioned too far forward in stance
  • Weight on back foot instead of forward
  • Trying to "scoop" or help the ball up
  • Hands behind the ball at impact
  • Stance too wide

The Fix

  1. Setup: Move ball back to center of stance
  2. Weight: Put 70% of weight on front foot and KEEP IT THERE
  3. Hands: Position hands ahead of ball at address
  4. Narrow stance: Feet only 6-8 inches apart
  5. Thought: "Brush the grass after the ball"

Checkpoint Drill

Make practice swings brushing a tee lying on the ground. Your divot should start where the tee is, not before it. Then place a ball where the tee was.

Recommended Practice Drill

Work on Drill 1: Consistent Strike Drill above. This drill specifically addresses hand action that causes chunking.

The Problem

Leading edge strikes the ball's equator, causing it to shoot low and race across the green.

Most Common Causes

  • Standing up during the swing (raising up)
  • Ball positioned too far back in stance
  • Too much weight on back foot
  • Trying to "scoop" the ball (ironically causes thin shots too)
  • Spine angle changes during swing
  • Not enough knee bend at address

The Fix

  1. Setup: Ball position center of stance
  2. Posture: Bend knees comfortably, maintain spine angle
  3. Weight: 60% on front foot
  4. Key thought: "Keep your head still" - don't stand up
  5. Feeling: Stay down through the shot, finish low

Wall Drill

Practice with a wall or chair behind your head. Set up with your head touching it. Make chips without losing contact with the wall - this prevents standing up.

Recommended Practice Drill

Work on Drill 3: Chip Within 3 Feet. The precision required will force you to make solid contact, not thin contact.

The Problem

Sometimes you chunk it, sometimes you blade it. Results are unpredictable.

Root Cause

Inconsistent contact stems from too much hand/wrist action and lack of body rotation. Your hands are too active and timing varies.

The Fix: Simplify Your Motion

  1. Lock in setup: Create the same setup every time
    • Narrow stance
    • Weight forward
    • Hands ahead
    • Ball center
  2. Quiet hands: Minimize wrist action
  3. Rotate shoulders: Chip with your body, not your hands
  4. Same tempo: Count "1-2" for every chip

Pre-Shot Routine

Develop a routine you use for EVERY chip:

  1. Pick landing spot
  2. Select club based on carry:roll ratio
  3. Take one practice swing feeling the distance
  4. Step in, set weight forward, hands ahead
  5. Look at landing spot
  6. Chip with quiet hands and shoulder rotation

The Problem

Contact is solid, but you're leaving chips short or blasting them past the hole. Distance control is inconsistent.

Most Common Causes

  • Wrong club selection for the situation
  • Not understanding carry vs. roll ratios
  • Decelerating through impact
  • Backswing length varies randomly
  • No landing spot selected before the shot

The Fix: Master Club Selection

  1. Learn the ratios:
    • 7-iron: 20% carry / 80% roll
    • PW: 50% carry / 50% roll
    • LW: 80% carry / 20% roll
  2. Pick your landing spot: Know exactly where you want the ball to land
  3. Choose the right club: Based on how much green you have
  4. Commit to the shot: Once you've picked club and spot, commit fully

Distance Control Formula

Total Distance to Hole = Landing Spot Distance + Expected Roll
Example with PW (50/50): 30 feet to hole = 15 feet carry + 15 feet roll

Technique for Consistent Distance

  • Backswing length: Controls distance (not speed)
  • Smooth tempo: Same speed for all chips
  • Accelerate through: Never decelerate
  • Practice feeling: Take practice swings feeling the distance
