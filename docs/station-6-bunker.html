Station 6: Bunker Play

Build confidence from sand in all situations
Start with Regular Bunker Shot: Master the fundamentals before progressing to specialty situations. The basic technique forms the foundation for all other bunker shots.

All 9 Bunker Situations

This is your starting point. The basics to the typical bunker shots are covered and a lot of the tips will apply to other bunker variations. Master these fundamentals before moving to specialty shots.

Key Fundamentals:

  • Club Selection: Use sand wedge with bounce (56-58 degrees)
  • Entry Point: Hit sand 2 inches behind ball
  • Setup: Open clubface and open stance
  • Swing: Accelerate through the sand
  • Follow Through: Complete your finish

Setup Details:

  • Open stance 20-30 degrees left of target
  • Open clubface to increase loft and bounce
  • Weight slightly forward (60/40)
  • Ball position forward of center
  • Dig feet into sand for stability

Execution:

  • Focus on spot 2 inches behind ball
  • Take a full, aggressive swing
  • Let the club slide under the ball
  • Maintain acceleration through impact
  • Finish with chest facing target

Expected Ball Flight:

High, soft landing with minimal roll. The ball should land on the green and stop quickly.

The Challenge: Wet or compacted sand creates a firm surface. The club can bounce off the hard surface and shoot your ball clean over the green without proper technique adjustments.

Key Adjustments from Regular Shot:

  • Club Selection: Use lob wedge (60-64°) instead of sand wedge - less bounce required
  • Entry Point: Aim 1 inch behind ball (not 2 inches)
  • Contact: More precise contact required due to firm surface
  • Bounce: Reduce bounce engagement to prevent skulling

Setup Changes:

  • Less open clubface than normal
  • Slightly steeper angle of attack
  • Ball position middle to slightly forward
  • Weight more forward (65/35)

Execution Tips:

  • Focus on spot 1 inch behind ball (closer than normal)
  • Steeper swing path to penetrate firm sand
  • Don't try to help the ball up - trust the loft
  • Accelerate through impact
Common Mistake: Using too much bounce on compact sand causes the club to bounce into the ball, sending it over the green.

The Challenge: Your golf ball lands into a firm bunker and digs in rather than bounce off. The goal is to successfully dislodge the ball from its tricky buried location.

Key Adjustments:

  • Clubface: Square the clubface (NOT open) - this is the opposite of normal
  • Entry Point: Aim 3 inches behind ball (farther than normal)
  • Attack Angle: Much steeper angle of attack required
  • Swing: Aggressive, powerful swing needed
  • Result: Expect less spin and more roll on landing

Setup:

  • Square or slightly closed clubface
  • Narrower stance for steeper attack
  • Weight significantly forward (70/30)
  • Ball position middle of stance
  • Hands well ahead of ball

Execution:

  • Pick the club up steeply on backswing
  • Drive the leading edge down into sand behind ball
  • Hit down aggressively - no finesse here
  • Don't expect a full follow-through
  • Club will dig into sand and stop

Expected Outcome:

Ball will come out lower with less spin. Plan for significant roll after landing. Aim short of your target to account for extra roll.

Key Difference: This is the only bunker shot where you CLOSE the clubface instead of opening it.

The Challenge: Your golf ball lands into a fluffy bunker and stays inside the crater it created, surrounded by a ring of sand like a fried egg. Goal is to avoid hitting the ball thin or leaving it in the bunker.

Key Technique:

  • Target: Aim for the back edge of the crater (where ball meets back wall)
  • Setup: Similar to regular bunker shot but slightly more aggressive
  • Execution: Focus on center contact through the fluffy sand
  • Swing: Full, committed swing required

Setup:

  • Open clubface moderately
  • Open stance 20-30 degrees
  • Weight forward (60/40)
  • Ball position slightly forward of center

Execution:

  • Aim for back edge of the crater
  • Take normal swing length
  • Accelerate through the fluffy sand
  • Don't decelerate - fluffy sand requires speed
  • Complete your follow-through

Managing Fluffy Sand:

Fluffy sand offers less resistance than firm sand. The ball will come out with reasonable trajectory and moderate spin. Plan for medium roll after landing.

Tip: Fried-egg lies are actually easier than they look. The fluffy sand around the ball helps create the explosion needed to get the ball out.

The Challenge: Ball lands or rolls up the side of the bunker closest to the green. Without modifications, your club will dig into the sand behind the ball and get stuck there.

Key Adjustments:

  • Spine Angle: Match your spine angle to the slope - tilt back with the hill
  • Ball Position: Play ball more forward in stance
  • Swing Path: Swing along the slope (upward)
  • Trajectory: Expect higher ball flight than normal
  • Distance: Ball will fly shorter due to added loft

Setup:

  • Set shoulders parallel to slope (back shoulder lower)
  • Weight distribution feels even (but more on back foot)
  • Ball position forward - opposite high foot
  • Open clubface as normal
  • Widen stance for stability on slope

Execution:

  • Maintain spine angle throughout swing
  • Swing up the slope - resist urge to dig down
  • Let slope add loft naturally
  • Accept limited follow-through
  • Ball will launch high and soft

Distance Control:

Ball will fly 10-20% shorter than from a level lie due to increased effective loft. Take a longer swing or use more club to compensate.

Key Principle: Match your spine to the slope. This prevents the club from digging in and allows you to swing along the natural plane of the slope.

The Challenge: Ball resting up the side of the bunker farthest away from the green. Without adjustments, you risk skulling the ball thin and sending it over the green. This is the most difficult bunker shot.

Key Adjustments:

  • Spine Angle: Match spine angle to slope - tilt forward with the hill
  • Ball Position: Play ball back in stance
  • Swing Path: Swing down along the slope
  • Trajectory: Expect lower ball flight than normal
  • Roll: Ball will run significantly more after landing

Setup:

  • Set shoulders parallel to slope (front shoulder lower)
  • More weight on front foot (65/35)
  • Ball position back of center - opposite low foot
  • Open clubface MORE than normal to add loft
  • Widen stance for stability

Execution:

  • Maintain forward spine tilt throughout
  • Swing down the slope - follow the terrain
  • Accept that ball will come out low
  • Limited follow-through is normal
  • Ball will release significantly on landing

Distance and Roll Management:

Ball will fly lower and roll much farther than normal. Aim well short of your target. This shot requires more precision and experience to execute successfully.

Most Difficult Shot: Downhill bunker shots are extremely challenging. Practice these extensively before attempting on the course. When in doubt, aim for the safe side of the green.

The Challenge: Ball sits far from the flag on a large green. Goal is to send the ball up onto the green and rolling towards the hole rather than stopping it quickly.

Key Adjustments:

  • Swing Length: Longer backswing for increased distance
  • Acceleration: Maintain acceleration through impact
  • Contact Point: Still hit sand first, but closer to ball (1.5 inches vs 2 inches)
  • Club Selection: Consider pitching wedge or gap wedge instead of sand wedge
  • Follow Through: Full, complete follow-through

Setup:

  • Less open stance than normal bunker shot
  • Slightly less open clubface
  • Weight distribution more even (55/45)
  • Ball position center to slightly forward

Club Selection Guide:

  • 20-30 yards: Sand wedge with longer swing
  • 30-40 yards: Gap wedge or pitching wedge
  • 40+ yards: 9-iron or less lofted club

Execution:

  • Make longer, fuller swing than normal bunker shot
  • Contact point slightly closer to ball (1-1.5" behind)
  • Accelerate through the sand aggressively
  • Complete full follow-through
  • Ball will come out lower with more roll
Distance Secret: Distance comes from swing length and club selection, not from trying to hit the ball directly. Still take sand first, just less of it.

The Challenge: Minimal green space between bunker and hole. Goal is to make the ball fly a short distance and land softly onto the green with minimal roll.

Key Adjustments:

  • Swing Length: Shorter backswing (half swing or less)
  • Acceleration: Maintain FULL acceleration despite shorter swing
  • Clubface: Very open clubface for maximum loft
  • Trajectory: High, soft landing required
  • Touch: Delicate feel and tempo needed

Setup:

  • Extremely open clubface (face points to sky)
  • Very open stance (30-40 degrees left)
  • Weight significantly forward (65/35)
  • Ball position forward in stance
  • Narrow stance for control

Execution:

  • Shorter backswing - wrists to waist height
  • Aim 2 inches behind ball (normal distance)
  • Accelerate through - don't decelerate!
  • Let the loft do the work
  • Ball will pop up high and soft

Face Angle Management:

The key to short bunker shots is maximum loft. Open the face until it points nearly straight up. This extreme loft combined with acceleration creates the high, soft landing you need.

Common Mistake: Decelerating because of the short distance. You must accelerate through the sand even on short shots - the open face and short swing control distance.

The Challenge: Bunkers located in the fairway or beyond greenside areas. IMPORTANT: The advice for greenside bunkers DOES NOT apply here. Distance requirements change the fundamentals completely.

Key Differences from Greenside Bunkers:

  • Contact: Hit ball FIRST (not sand first) - this is opposite of greenside
  • Club Selection: Use less lofted club for distance (5-iron through 9-iron)
  • Clean Contact: Essential - any fat contact kills distance
  • Goal: Get out and advance ball toward target
  • Lip Height: Choose club that clears the lip safely

Setup:

  • Normal stance - square to target (NOT open)
  • Normal clubface - square (NOT open)
  • Ball position slightly back of normal
  • Dig feet in slightly for stability
  • Choke down 1 inch on grip

Club Selection Strategy:

  • Assess lip height first - can you clear it?
  • Take MORE loft than you think (safety first)
  • If in doubt, take one extra club of loft
  • Better to get out safely than hit perfect and catch the lip

Execution:

  • Quiet lower body - minimize leg action
  • Focus on ball-first contact
  • Swing smoothly - 80% effort
  • Avoid excessive weight shift
  • Pick the ball clean off the sand

Priority Hierarchy:

1st Priority: Get out of the bunker
2nd Priority: Advance the ball forward
3rd Priority: Reach your target

Critical Rule: Ball first, not sand first. This is the complete opposite of greenside bunker technique. Any fat contact in a fairway bunker is disastrous.

Quick Fixes

Having trouble? Tap to see common problems and instant solutions.

Problem:

Ball doesn't make it out of the bunker - stays in the sand or barely moves.

Most Common Causes:

  • Deceleration: Slowing down through impact
  • Too much sand: Entering too far behind the ball (3+ inches)
  • Weak swing: Not enough speed to displace the sand
  • Digging too deep: Leading edge going down instead of through

Instant Fix:

  • Commit to acceleration: Swing like the ball is 30 yards away, not 10
  • Check entry point: Aim 2 inches behind (draw a line in practice)
  • Open the face more: Bounce should glide through sand, not dig
  • Complete the follow-through: Finish with chest facing target

Practice Drill:

Draw Lines Drill: Draw a line in the sand 2 inches before the ball and 4 inches after. Practice taking a divot that starts at the first line and extends past the second line. Focus on accelerating through the entire sand divot.

Key Principle: In bunkers, speed is your friend. Deceleration is the enemy. Make an aggressive, accelerating swing every time.

Problem:

Leading edge hits the center of the ball, sending it screaming across the green (or over it).

Most Common Causes:

  • Hitting ball first: Not entering sand behind the ball
  • Closed clubface: Leading edge exposed instead of bounce
  • Rising up: Body lifting through impact
  • Ball too far back: Hitting on the downswing before sand

Instant Fix:

  • Open the clubface MORE: Expose the bounce, hide the leading edge
  • Focus on the sand spot: Look at spot 2 inches behind ball, not at ball
  • Stay in your posture: Maintain spine angle through impact
  • Move ball forward: Play ball slightly more forward in stance

Practice Drill:

No-Ball Drill: Practice bunker swings without a ball. Focus on taking consistent sand divots 4-6 inches long starting 2 inches in front of where the ball would be. Once you can do this consistently, add the ball back.

Visual Tip: Never let your eyes move to the ball. Keep your focus locked on the sand entry point 2 inches behind the ball throughout the entire swing.

Problem:

Results vary wildly - sometimes perfect, sometimes fat, sometimes thin. No consistency from shot to shot.

Most Common Causes:

  • Moving lower body too much: Excessive leg action changes impact point
  • Looking up early: Head moving before impact
  • No consistent entry point: Hitting at different spots behind ball
  • Varying swing speed: Different tempos on different shots

Instant Fix:

  • Quiet the lower body: Feel like legs stay stable - arms and shoulders do the work
  • Pick a specific spot: Choose exact entry point and commit to it
  • Same tempo every time: Count "one-two" for every swing
  • Stay down through impact: Keep eyes on sand spot until after impact

Practice Drill:

Feet Together Drill: Practice bunker shots with feet together (6 inches apart). This forces you to quiet your lower body and use arms/shoulders. Once contact becomes consistent, gradually widen stance back to normal.

Consistency Checklist:

  • Same setup every time (stance, ball position, face angle)
  • Same entry point every time (2 inches behind)
  • Same tempo every time (smooth, rhythmic)
  • Same finish every time (chest facing target)
Consistency Secret: Bunker play is about repetitive technique, not feel. Create a checklist and follow it exactly on every shot.

Problem:

Ball is buried in the sand and you can't get it out consistently or at all.

Most Common Causes:

  • Clubface still open: Bounce prevents club from digging under ball
  • Not steep enough: Shallow attack angle won't penetrate to ball
  • Not aggressive enough: Tentative swing lacks power to extract ball
  • Wrong entry point: Not hitting far enough behind buried ball

Instant Fix:

  • SQUARE THE FACE: This is critical - close the face to remove bounce
  • Hit 3 inches behind: Farther behind than normal (vs 2 inches normally)
  • Steep angle of attack: Pick club up steeply, drive it down aggressively
  • Aggressive swing: This needs power - swing hard!
  • Don't expect follow-through: Club will bury in sand - that's correct

Proper Technique for Plugged Lies:

  • Square or slightly closed clubface (opposite of normal)
  • Narrower stance (easier to swing steeply)
  • Weight very forward (70/30)
  • Ball position middle of stance
  • Hands well ahead of ball at address
  • Pick club up steeply on backswing
  • Drive leading edge DOWN into sand
  • Accept that it will come out low with roll

Expected Result:

Ball will come out LOWER with MORE roll than normal bunker shot. Aim short of your target to compensate. This is NOT a finesse shot - it's a recovery shot.

Critical Technique Change: Plugged lies require a SQUARE or CLOSED clubface - the complete opposite of normal bunker technique. Don't try to use bounce on a plugged lie.

Recommended Learning Path

For Beginners:

  1. Start with Regular Bunker Shot - master the fundamentals
  2. Practice on level lies with normal sand conditions
  3. Build confidence with basic technique
  4. Then progress to Long and Short shots
  5. Finally tackle specialty situations (wet sand, slopes, plugged)

For Intermediate Players:

  1. Review Regular Bunker Shot technique
  2. Add Wet Sand and Long/Short shots to repertoire
  3. Practice Fairway Bunkers (different technique)
  4. Work on slope adjustments
  5. Develop feel for various sand conditions

For Advanced Players:

  1. Master all nine bunker shot types
  2. Practice from difficult lies regularly
  3. Develop ability to assess and adapt quickly
  4. Build confidence in any bunker situation
  5. Work on spin control and trajectory variations

Practice Tips

  • Spend time in practice bunkers regularly - don't avoid them
  • Start with basic shots before attempting difficult lies
  • Draw lines in sand to practice consistent contact point
  • Experiment with different clubs and techniques
  • Practice various distances and trajectories
  • Build confidence through repetition
  • Always practice with full acceleration - never decelerate
  • Create difficult lies intentionally to practice recovery
