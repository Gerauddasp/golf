Station 7: Putting

Session Goal
Build fundamentals and confidence on the greens

Core Drills

Purpose: Develop distance control and break management at key scoring distances.

Setup:

  • Position tees around holes at 1, 2, and 3 putter-lengths away
  • Create stations at each distance
  • Work around the hole clockwise
Layout: Balls positioned at 1, 2, and 3 putter lengths in circle around hole

Execution:

  • Start at first position (1 putter length)
  • Putt from each position moving clockwise around the hole
  • Make all putts at distance #1 before moving to distance #2
  • Adjust for break from different angles
  • Track makes at each distance

Goal: Master scoring distance (3-9 feet) by building confidence and touch at the most important putting distances.

This drill builds the foundation for consistent performance on short putts that save pars and make birdies.

Purpose: Reduce unwanted arc in the putting stroke for more consistent roll.

Setup:

  • Position two clubs parallel on either side of the ball
  • Create a channel for the putter to travel through
  • Clubs should be just wider than putter head
  • Use alignment sticks or shafts for consistency
Stroke path: Two parallel guides showing straight back and through motion

Key Focus:

  • Putter travels straight back and straight through
  • Eliminates excessive inside or outside arc
  • Promotes repeatable stroke path
  • Putter should not contact the guide clubs
  • Focus on shoulders rocking, not hands manipulating

Benefit: Fewer missed putts from poor stroke mechanics. A consistent path leads to predictable roll and better distance control.

Practice with short putts (3-6 feet) first, then gradually increase distance as path becomes consistent.

Purpose: Improve aiming precision with sharper target selection.

Setup:

  • Place a tee in the center of the cup
  • Aim to hit the tee rather than just "the hole"
  • Alternatively, pick a specific dimple or mark on back of cup
  • Practice from various distances (3-15 feet)
Aiming technique: Precise target (tee) in center of cup vs general cup target

Execution:

  • Pick your exact entry point on the cup
  • Visualize ball rolling to that specific spot
  • Aim for precision, not just "close enough"
  • Note where ball enters hole on successful putts

Key Principle: Training with a smaller, more precise target carries over to actual play. When you aim at a tee, the hole looks huge!

Benefit: Develops sharper visual focus and better aim on the course. Your subconscious becomes programmed for precision.

On the course, pick a specific blade of grass or discoloration as your target, not just the general direction.

Purpose: Emphasize pace control and three-putt prevention from long distances.

Setup:

  • Practice from long distances (30-60 feet)
  • Place markers at 3 feet around the hole (create a circle)
  • Use string or tees to mark the "safe zone"
Target zones: 3-foot circle around hole showing acceptable lag distance

Practice Goal:

  • Focus on stopping putts within three feet of the hole
  • Prioritize pace over holing out
  • Eliminate three-putts from long range
  • Build feel for different green speeds
  • Success = any ball finishing inside the circle

Execution:

  • Practice from various long distances
  • Focus on speed, not line (speed determines break)
  • Make practice strokes feeling the distance
  • Accept that holing long putts is bonus, not expectation

Benefit: Builds confidence on long putts and ensures easier second putts. Three-putts kill scores - this drill prevents them.

On uphill putts, imagine a 4-foot circle. On downhill putts, tighten to 2 feet for better control.

Purpose: Build confidence with aggressive pace that gives putts a chance to fall.

Practice Rule:

  • Every putt MUST reach the hole
  • Putts that stop short incur penalty distances
  • If ball stops short, move it 6 inches farther back for next attempt
  • Reinforces getting the ball to the hole
  • Builds aggressive mindset

Setup:

  • Practice from various distances (6-20 feet)
  • Mark original starting spot
  • Add penalty markers 6" farther back

Execution:

  • Make your normal stroke
  • Ball must reach or pass the hole
  • Short putts = restart from penalty spot
  • Goal: make 5 putts in a row from same spot

Key Principle: "Never up, never in" - putts that don't reach have zero chance. The back of the cup counts too!

Benefit: Eliminates tentative strokes and builds confident, aggressive putting. You'll be amazed how many "aggressive" putts catch the back of the cup.

Ideal speed: Ball would finish 12-18 inches past hole on a miss. This speed gives maximum capture area at the hole.

Green Reading Practice

How to Assess:

  • Walk around the hole viewing from all sides
  • Stand behind ball and behind hole for perspective
  • Look for the "low side" - water typically drains there
  • Check from 20 feet back for overall green tilt
  • Note if green slopes front-to-back or side-to-side

Key Principle: Every green has a dominant slope. Find it first, then look for subtle breaks.

Tips:

  • Greens typically slope toward water or lowest terrain
  • Most putts break more than you think
  • On doubt, trust the overall slope

Visualization Methods:

  • Apex Method: Pick the highest point of break, aim there
  • Track Method: Imagine ball's curved path to hole
  • Entry Point: Pick exact spot where ball enters cup
  • Speed Visualization: See ball rolling at correct pace

Practice Exercise:

  • Pick a breaking putt (6-10 feet)
  • Visualize the entire path before stroking
  • After stroking, watch if ball follows your visualization
  • Adjust mental image based on actual break
  • Repeat until visualization matches reality

Key Insight: Speed determines break. Faster putts break less, slower putts break more. Visualize speed and line together.

When Grain Matters:

  • Bermuda grass greens (common in warm climates)
  • Late afternoon when grass hasn't been mowed
  • Shiny appearance = downgrain (faster)
  • Dark/dull appearance = into grain (slower)

How to Read Grain:

  • Look at cup: grass growing over edge shows grain direction
  • Grain grows toward water and setting sun
  • Shiny = with grain (ball rolls farther)
  • Dull = against grain (ball rolls shorter)
  • Sideways grain affects break

Impact on Speed:

  • Downgrain: Hit softer (25% less force)
  • Into grain: Hit firmer (25% more force)
  • Cross-grain: Adds break in grain direction

On bent grass (northern courses), grain has minimal effect. Focus on slope instead.

Uphill Putts:

  • Hit more firmly - gravity slows ball
  • Ball will break LESS (higher speed through break)
  • Be aggressive - short putts don't go in
  • Larger margin for error past hole
  • Play less break than appears

Downhill Putts:

  • Hit softer - gravity accelerates ball
  • Ball will break MORE (slower speed = more break)
  • Focus on speed control first, then line
  • Smaller margin for error
  • Play more break than appears

Practice Tip: Hit 10 uphill putts followed by 10 downhill putts from same distance. Feel the difference in stroke length required.

Key Principle: Downhill is about speed. Uphill is about line. Master both separately.

On severe downhill putts, think "just get it rolling" and let gravity do the work.

How to Use Plumb Bob:

  • Stand behind ball, facing hole
  • Close one eye (your dominant eye stays open)
  • Hold putter lightly between thumb and finger
  • Let putter hang vertically (true vertical)
  • Align putter shaft so it covers the ball
  • Look at where shaft crosses the hole

Reading the Result:

  • If shaft covers hole = straight putt
  • If hole appears left of shaft = breaks left
  • If hole appears right of shaft = breaks right
  • Distance from shaft = amount of break

Limitations:

  • Only shows break along your sight line
  • Doesn't account for grain or green speed
  • Works best on consistent, moderate slopes
  • Should confirm, not replace, visual read

Best Practice: Use plumb bob as a confirmation tool when your eyes and feet disagree on break direction.

Many pros don't use this method. Trust your feet and eyes first - they feel the slope best.

Quick Fixes - Having Trouble?

Common Causes:

  • Alignment: Shoulders aimed left of target
  • Path: Outside-in stroke (putter going left through impact)
  • Face: Closed face at impact
  • Setup: Standing too far from ball, reaching

Quick Fixes:

  • Check shoulder alignment: Shoulders should be parallel to target line, not open
  • Practice gate drill: Place tees just outside putter path to ensure straight stroke
  • Feel square face: Practice with a dime on putter face - keep it from falling off
  • Setup adjustment: Stand slightly closer, eyes directly over ball

Recommended Drill: Straight Back and Through Drill (#2) with emphasis on shoulder rotation, not hand manipulation.

Most pulls come from shoulders, not hands. Check alignment first!

Common Causes:

  • Alignment: Shoulders aimed right of target
  • Path: Inside-out stroke (putter going right through impact)
  • Face: Open face at impact
  • Setup: Ball too far forward in stance

Quick Fixes:

  • Check shoulder alignment: Ensure shoulders parallel to target line
  • Ball position: Move ball slightly back (center of stance or just forward)
  • Grip pressure: Lighten grip - tension causes pushing
  • Release drill: Feel putter face rotating through impact, not staying open

Recommended Drill: Target Center of Cup Drill (#3) - pick a precise target to improve face aim.

Practice Check: Place alignment stick parallel to target line. Check if your shoulders, hips, and putter face are all square.

Pushes often come from trying to "guide" the ball. Trust your stroke and let it flow!

Common Causes:

  • Backswing too long: Then slowing down to compensate
  • Fear of going past hole: Especially on downhill putts
  • Steering the ball: Trying to guide rather than stroke
  • Tension: Tight grip restricts natural flow

Quick Fixes:

  • Shorten backswing: Take putter back only half as far
  • Accelerate through: Follow-through should be longer than backswing
  • Practice tempo: 1-2 rhythm (short back, smooth through)
  • Lighten grip pressure: Hold putter like a bird - firm but not crushing

Key Principle: Short back, smooth through. The follow-through tells the ball where to go.

Recommended Drill: Don't Leave It Short Drill (#5) - forces you to accelerate through impact.

Practice Drill: Hit 10 putts where follow-through is 2x backswing length. Feel the acceleration!

Deceleration causes more short putts misses than anything else. Commit to the stroke!

Common Causes:

  • Inconsistent tempo: Different speeds on different putts
  • No practice strokes: Not feeling the distance
  • Poor contact: Hitting toe or heel of putter
  • Not reading green speed: Every green is different

Quick Fixes:

  • Establish tempo: Count "1-2" on every putt (same rhythm)
  • Take practice strokes: Looking at hole, feel the distance in your body
  • Sweet spot contact: Mark sweet spot, focus on center hits
  • Green speed test: Hit first putt from 30 feet to gauge speed

Recommended Drills:

  • Lag Putting Drill (#4): Build feel for long distances
  • Eyes-closed drill: Putt with eyes closed after seeing hole - develops feel

Distance Control Formula:

  • Consistent tempo + sweet spot contact + practice strokes = great speed control

Speed is more important than line. A putt with perfect speed and average line goes in more than perfect line with poor speed.

Common Causes:

  • Peeking: Looking up too soon to see result
  • Not reading break: Assuming short putts are straight
  • Timid stroke: Babying it instead of confident stroke
  • Poor routine: Rushing because "it's short"

Quick Fixes:

  • Listen for the drop: Don't look up until you hear ball drop
  • Read every putt: Even 3-footers can break - always read them
  • Confident stroke: Short putts need firm stroke (12-18" past hole)
  • Same routine: Treat 3-footer like any other putt - full routine

Mental Keys:

  • Pick a spot at back of cup - be specific
  • Trust your read - commit to the line
  • Accelerate through - no deceleration on short putts
  • Stay in posture until ball drops

Recommended Drills:

  • Clockwise 1-2-3 Drill (#1): Perfect for building confidence at short range
  • Target Center of Cup (#3): Precise aiming eliminates doubt

Pressure Practice: Make 10 three-footers in a row. Miss = start over. Builds confidence under pressure.

Short putts are not "gimmes" until they're in the hole. Give them the respect they deserve!
