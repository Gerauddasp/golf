Scoring Goals

üéØ Breaking 100 (Target: 90-99)

Core Philosophy: Avoid penalties at all costs. Don't count score mid-round.

Target Scores by Hole
Par 3Bogey (4)1 shot on/near green + 2 putts + buffer
Short Par 4 (<320m)Bogey (5)2 shots to green + 2 putts + buffer
Long Par 4 (365m+)Double (6)3 x 130m + chip + 2 putts
Par 5Double (7)4 shots to green + 2 putts + buffer

The Math: 9 bogeys + 9 doubles = 99

Key Rules
  • Bogey = win, double bogey = OK
  • Two-putt everything - work to eliminate 3-putts
  • Leave driver at home - use hybrid or 6-iron that goes 135m+ straight
  • Divide every hole into 130m segments

Core Philosophy: Master five essential shots. Course management beats swing changes.

  • Use your most consistent long club (145m in fairway beats 200m in trees)
üéØ Breaking 80: The 666 Method (Target: 70-79)

Core Philosophy: Built-in error tolerance. You don't need to be perfect.

The 666 Framework
6 Greens in RegulationOnly need to hit 1/3 of greens
6 Up-and-DownsSave par from missed greens
6 "Birthday" HolesAllow mistakes - doubles are OK

The Math: 6 GIR pars + 6 scramble pars + 6 bogeys/doubles = 78-79

Stats to Hit
  • 50%+ scrambling (up-and-down rate)
  • Zero three-putts
  • Make 19 of 20 putts inside 1.5m
  • Plan from green backwards - what approach distance do you want?
üéØ Breaking 70 (Target: Under Par)

Core Philosophy: Breaking par imperfectly. Eliminate big errors, don't chase perfection.

The Math: 2-3 birdies + 12-14 pars + 2-3 bogeys + 0-1 double = 69 or better

Stats to Hit
  • 12+ Greens in Regulation
  • 65%+ scrambling
  • Zero three-putts
  • Create 4-6 birdie opportunities per round
  • Max 1 double bogey
Think Box / Play Box
  • Think Box (behind ball): Assess everything, make all decisions
  • Decision Line: Cross it = 100% committed
  • Play Box (at ball): Zero doubt, blank mind, execute
  • If doubt appears: BACK AWAY and restart

üéØ Short Game Fundamentals

Putting

üéØ Putting Fundamentals

Lag 6m+ putts: Get within 1m circle of hole

Speed > Line: Speed determines break - get speed right first

Uphill: Hit firm - plays less break than you think

Downhill: Speed control first - plays more break
