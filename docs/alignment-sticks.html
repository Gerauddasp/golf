Alignment Sticks

TRAIN YOUR EYE
6 essential drills to fix the #1 amateur mistake
Why This Matters

High handicappers have DOUBLE the alignment errors of pros. You can't hit a target you're not aimed at.

  • Most golfers aim 10-20 yards off target without knowing
  • Alignment is a practiced skill, not natural instinct
  • 10 minutes with sticks = better aim for weeks

Setup & Alignment Drills

1. Train Track Drill

Purpose: Establish proper parallel alignment for every shot

Setup 2 sticks parallel, shoulder-width apart
Position Ball on target line, feet on body line
How To
  1. Place one stick pointing at target (target line)
  2. Place second stick parallel, where your feet will be (body line)
  3. Hit 10 shots, checking feet stay parallel to body line
  4. Notice: body line aims LEFT of target (for right-handers)
Success: Feet, hips, and shoulders all parallel to target line - not aimed at target.
2. Ball Position Drill

Purpose: Consistent ball position for every club while maintaining alignment

Setup 2 sticks: 1 on target line + 1 perpendicular
Position Perpendicular stick through ball position
How To
  1. Place first stick pointing at target (target line)
  2. Place second stick perpendicular, running through ball
  3. Take stance - perpendicular stick shows ball position relative to feet
  4. Check: Driver at front heel, wedges at center
  5. Hit 5 shots per club, keeping ball at correct position
Success: Same ball position for same club, every time - while staying aligned to target.

Swing Path Drills

3. Swing Plane Check

Purpose: Train proper backswing plane

Setup 1 stick in ground at 45 degrees behind ball
Angle Matches your shaft angle at address
How To
  1. Stick angled 45 degrees, pointing at your belt
  2. Position so club should miss stick on backswing
  3. Make slow backswings - club should stay below stick
  4. If you hit stick, you're too flat or lifting arms
Tip: At the range, put stick through empty ball basket if you can't stick it in ground.
4. Target Line Gate

Purpose: Prevent over-the-top move, promote inside path

Setup 2 sticks creating gate just outside ball
Width Club head width + 5cm each side
How To
  1. Place 2 sticks vertically, creating gate on target line
  2. Gate should be just wider than club head
  3. Swing through without hitting sticks
  4. If you hit outside stick = over the top
Success: Club passes through gate cleanly, promoting square or inside path.

Strike Quality Drills

5. Low Point Drill

Purpose: Consistent low point ahead of ball for crisp contact

Setup 1 stick flat on ground, 5cm ahead of ball
Goal Divot starts at or after the stick
How To
  1. Lay stick on ground perpendicular to target line
  2. Position 5cm target-side of ball
  3. Hit shots - divot should start AT or AFTER stick
  4. If divot starts before stick = hitting fat
Success: Ball-first contact, divot ahead of original ball position.
6. Putting Gate

Purpose: Square putter face and straight stroke

Setup 2 sticks parallel on green, putter-width apart
Length 60cm "track" for stroke to travel through
How To
  1. Create narrow channel just wider than putter head
  2. Ball at center of track
  3. Stroke without touching either stick
  4. If you hit a stick, path is off-line
Success: 10 strokes in a row without touching the sticks.
Quick Session Guide
5 min warmup Train Track Drill (10 shots)
Full swing focus Add Target Line Gate
Iron work Add Low Point Drill
Putting warmup Putting Gate (20 strokes)
