ESCAPE THE SAND
3 shots for any bunker situation

Quick Reference

Shot When Face Entry
Regular Good lie Open 5cm behind
Plugged Buried ball Square 10cm behind
Distance Long/Short Varies Swing length
The One Rule: Always accelerate through the sand. Never decelerate - that's how you leave it in the bunker.

Distance Control

Distance Face Swing
Short (<10m) Very open Half swing
Medium (10-20m) Normal open Full swing
Long (20m+) Less open Longer swing
Key: Control distance with swing length and face angle, not swing speed. Same acceleration always.

Practice Exercises

Escape Drill
Setup10 balls in bunker, good lies
GoalAll 10 out in one shot
Success10/10 on the green
Success: Never take 2 shots to escape the sand
Up and Down Challenge
SetupBunker shot + putt out
Reps9 attempts (3 per hole)
GoalComplete 3/9 sand saves
Success: 33% sand save rate is respectable for amateurs
Line Drill
SetupDraw line in sand, no ball
Reps10 swings hitting the line
GoalConsistent entry point
Note: Practice hitting the same spot every time. Add ball when entry is consistent.

Quick Fixes

Having trouble? Tap to see solutions.

Cause: Deceleration, hitting too far behind ball, weak swing

Fix:

  1. Commit to accelerating through - swing like it's 25m away
  2. Check entry point - should be 5cm behind, not 8+cm
  3. Open the face MORE to expose bounce
  4. Complete your follow-through to the target

Cause: Hitting ball first, closed face, rising up

Fix:

  1. Open the clubface MORE (expose the bounce)
  2. Focus on spot 5cm BEHIND the ball, not the ball
  3. Stay in your posture - don't lift up
  4. Move ball forward in stance

Cause: Using open face (normal technique doesn't work on plugged lies)

Fix:

  1. SQUARE the clubface - opposite of normal!
  2. Hit 10cm behind (farther than normal)
  3. Steep angle - pick club up and drive it DOWN
  4. Swing hard - this needs power, not finesse
  5. Accept: ball comes out low with lots of roll
