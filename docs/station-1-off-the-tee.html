Off the Tee

The Goal is STRAIGHT
Master two shots: High Tee for distance, Low Tee for control
Two Shots to Master
High Tee Standard shot for max distance 60m+ landing zone
Low Tee Control shot for tight spots & wind <60m landing zone

Both shots prioritize hitting the fairway. Distance is secondary to accuracy.

High Tee Shot

When to Use
  • Wide fairway (60m+ landing zone)
  • No trouble in play
  • Need maximum carry distance
  • Tailwind conditions
Pre-Shot Routine
  1. Stand behind ball, pick specific target
  2. Visualize ball flight (high, straight)
  3. One practice swing for tempo
  4. Address ball, deep breath
  5. Commit 100% - if doubt, step back
Setup Keys
Ball Position Forward, off left heel
Tee Height Ball center at crown level
Stance Wide (5-15cm beyond shoulders)
Weight 60/40 on back foot
Spine Tilt Away from target
Grip Light - "hold like a bird"
Swing Dynamic
  • Power: 95% - not 100%
  • Tempo: Smooth, controlled
  • Motion: Sweep up through the ball
  • Finish: Belt buckle to target, balanced
Remember: "Swing hard, not too hard." Consistency beats distance every time.

Low Tee Shot

When to Use
  • Tight landing zone (<60m width)
  • Hazards in play (water, OB)
  • Strong headwind or crosswind
  • Need to keep ball under obstacles
  • Position over distance required
Pre-Shot Routine
  1. Stand behind ball, pick SAFE target
  2. Visualize ball flight (lower, controlled)
  3. One practice swing - compact finish
  4. Address ball, deep breath
  5. Commit 100% - smooth tempo
Setup Keys
Ball Position Slightly back of normal
Tee Height Lower - half ball above crown
Stance Slightly narrower than high tee
Weight More centered (55/45)
Grip Choke down 2-3cm for control
Club Choice Consider 3-wood or hybrid
Swing Dynamic
  • Power: 80-85% - control is priority
  • Tempo: Even smoother than high tee
  • Motion: More level through impact
  • Finish: Compact, controlled finish
Remember: 145m in the fairway beats 200m in the trees. Every time.

Trackman Practice

1. Dispersion Test

Purpose: Know your miss pattern - see your left-right spread

ModePractice
Balls10 shots
WatchLateral Dispersion + "Jellybean" view
How To
  1. Select Practice from main menu
  2. Pick a target at your distance (180-220m)
  3. Hit 10 balls with your stock shot
  4. Check the Jellybean view for your pattern
Success: Tight cluster with predictable miss direction. Note: small circle = tour average, large circle = 15-handicap.
2. Bullseye

Purpose: Train precision under competitive pressure

ModeGames → Bullseye
Balls10 shots per round
WatchDistance from Target, Points scored
How To
  1. Select Games → Bullseye
  2. Closer to center = more points
  3. Further targets = bonus points
  4. Compete against yourself or friends
Success: Beat your previous high score. Track improvement over sessions.
3. On the Edge

Purpose: Master distance control - hit target WITHOUT going over

ModeGames → On the Edge
BallsKnockout format
WatchCarry Distance consistency
How To
  1. Select Games → On the Edge
  2. Get as close to target distance as possible
  3. Go OVER the target = eliminated
  4. Teaches: controlled 95% swings, not max effort
Success: Survive multiple rounds. Proves you control your carry distance.

Quick Fixes - Having Trouble?

Cause

Ball starts left and curves right due to outside-in club path with clubface open relative to path. Common causes include open stance, weak grip, and over-the-top move.

Quick Fix

  • Stance: Check feet are parallel to target line (square stance, not open)
  • Grip: Strengthen grip - rotate hands so you see 2-3 knuckles on left hand
  • Path: Take club back square, avoid pushing hands away from body at top
  • Release: Allow natural rotation of hands through impact

Cause

Ball starts right and curves aggressively left due to inside-out path with clubface closed relative to path. Often caused by closed stance, strong grip, or excessive inside takeaway.

Quick Fix

  • Stance: Adjust to square stance (feet parallel to target, not closed/right)
  • Grip: Weaken grip by rotating hands to show fewer knuckles
  • Takeaway: Ensure square takeaway, not too far inside
  • Top Position: Keep left wrist flat, not bowed forward
  • Release: Control hand rotation through impact

Cause

Club hits ball above equator when swing arc bottom is too high, too early (before ball), or too late (after ball). Often caused by swaying, reverse pivot, standing up at impact, or poor ball position.

Quick Fix

  • Avoid Swaying: Keep right knee leaned in and flexed throughout backswing
  • Weight Transfer: Shift weight to back foot in backswing, front foot in downswing (not reverse)
  • Ball Position: Start with ball in center of stance, adjust from there
  • Setup: Distribute weight equally, let right shoulder sit naturally lower
  • Head Position: Keep head at same height from address through impact

Cause

Leading edge strikes ball below equator when swing arc bottom is too high or positioned after ball. Common causes include standing up at impact, swaying, scooping/flipping, or ball positioned too far forward.

Quick Fix

  • Keep Head in Box: Maintain same head height from address through impact
  • No Swaying: Keep right knee leaned in and flexed
  • No Scooping: Keep wrists from releasing angle through impact
  • Ball Position: Check ball isn't too far forward in stance
  • Spine Angle: Maintain consistent forward tilt throughout swing

Cause

Distance loss often results from poor contact (fat/thin shots), insufficient swing speed, early release (casting), incomplete shoulder turn, or incorrect club selection/setup.

Quick Fix

  • Contact First: Focus on solid center-face strikes before swinging harder
  • Full Turn: Complete shoulder rotation at top of backswing
  • Maintain Lag: Keep wrist hinge through transition, release at bottom
  • Tempo: Smooth tempo often produces more distance than rushing
  • Equipment: Ensure proper tee height and ball position for driver
