Station 5: Pitching

Master distance control from 10-70m with consistent trajectory and landing spot precision

Swing Length Reference

Throughout this guide, we describe swing lengths by hand position:

  • Quarter swing - Hands reach hip/waist height
  • Half swing - Hands reach chest height
  • Three-quarter swing - Hands reach shoulder height

Distance Control by Yardage

Setup Adjustments

  • Stance: Narrow stance - feet closer together than shoulders for enhanced feel and rotation
  • Ball Position: Center to slightly back in stance
  • Weight Distribution: 60/40 favoring front foot
  • Hands: Slightly forward at address, creating gentle forward shaft lean
  • Grip Pressure: Light and sensitive for maximum feel

Swing Length

  • Backswing: Quarter swing - hands reach hip height maximum
  • Follow-through: Match backswing length for consistency
  • Tempo: Smooth and controlled, no need to rush
  • Key Feel: Think of a longer putting stroke with shoulders and chest rotation

Club Selection

  • Sand Wedge (54-56°): Default choice for most situations
  • Lob Wedge (58-60°): When you need extra height or soft landing
  • Landing Spot: Aim to land 2/3 of the way to hole, letting ball release
  • Roll Ratio: Expect 1:1 carry-to-roll ratio with SW, less with LW

1/4 Swing Technique

  • Backswing Length: Hands reach between hip and waist height
  • Wrist Hinge: Minimal hinge - feel mostly shoulder turn
  • Body Rotation: Slight shoulder turn, quiet lower body
  • Acceleration: Maintain steady pace through impact, no deceleration
  • Follow-through: Natural release matching backswing length

Landing Spot Focus

  • Spot Selection: Pick specific landing area on green
  • Visualization: See ball landing and releasing to hole
  • Commitment: Trust your distance and swing to landing spot
  • Green Reading: Account for slope after landing
  • Practice Drill: Place towel at landing spot, try to hit it consistently

Trajectory Options

  • Standard Height (PW/GW 48-52°): Balanced flight with moderate roll
  • Higher Flight (SW 54-56°): More carry, less roll, softer landing
  • Maximum Height (LW 58-60°): Steep descent, minimal roll
  • Club Selection Strategy: Use less lofted club for more roll, more lofted for spin
Key Thought: Control distance with swing length, not swing speed. Maintain consistent tempo regardless of club choice.

1/2 Swing Technique

  • Backswing Length: Hands reach chest height, arms parallel to ground
  • Wrist Hinge: Natural hinge begins, about 45-60 degrees
  • Weight Shift: Slight weight shift to back foot on backswing
  • Downswing: Weight transfers forward, hands lead clubhead through impact
  • Finish Position: Balanced follow-through, hands chest height

Club Selection Strategy

  • Gap Wedge (50-52°): 50-50m range with half swing
  • Sand Wedge (54-56°): 35-50m range with half swing
  • Lob Wedge (58-60°): 25-35m range with half swing
  • Distance Gaps: Know your personal yardages for each wedge at this swing length
  • Practice Routine: Hit 10 balls with each wedge, measure average distance

Spin Management

  • Maximum Spin Setup: Clean ball, fresh grooves, square impact, full finish
  • Reduced Spin Setup: Ball slightly back, hands forward, shorter follow-through
  • Conditions Impact: Wet conditions = less spin; firm conditions = more spin needed
  • Green Firmness: Soft greens allow spin, hard greens require landing spot calculation
  • Technique Factor: Descending blow creates spin; clean contact is essential

3/4 Swing Technique

  • Backswing Length: Hands reach shoulder height, club shaft parallel to ground
  • Full Wrist Hinge: Complete wrist cock, 90-degree angle
  • Shoulder Turn: 3/4 shoulder rotation, not full turn
  • Weight Transfer: Full weight shift back and through
  • Impact Position: Hands ahead of ball, descending blow, ball-first contact
  • Follow-through: Complete release, finish with chest facing target

Distance Gaps Between Clubs

  • Pitching Wedge (46-48°): 60-70m with 3/4 swing
  • Gap Wedge (50-52°): 50-60m with 3/4 swing
  • Sand Wedge (54-56°): 50-50m with 3/4 swing
  • Gap Pattern: Expect 10-10m gaps between wedges
  • Your Numbers: Practice to establish YOUR personal distance gaps
  • Consistency Key: Same swing length for each club = predictable results

Wind Considerations

  • Headwind: Take 1-2 more clubs, lower trajectory, more roll
  • Tailwind: Take 1 less club, expect extra carry and roll
  • Crosswind: Aim into wind, ball will drift back toward target
  • Strong Wind Strategy: Play lower trajectory shots with less lofted clubs
  • Trajectory Control: Ball back in stance reduces height in wind
  • Club Selection: More club + easier swing = better control than max effort
Pro Tip: In strong wind, pitch with a gap wedge using ball-back position rather than forcing a sand wedge. Lower flight = better control.

Trajectory Variations

When to Use

  • Over Obstacles: Bunkers, water, rough between you and green
  • Tight Pin Position: Flag close to your side with little green to work with
  • Soft Greens: Receptive surfaces that hold shots well
  • Elevated Green: Shooting uphill to green above you
  • Need Quick Stop: Ball must land softly and stop fast

How to Execute

  • Club Selection: Use most lofted wedge - lob wedge (58-60°) ideal
  • Ball Position: Center of stance - NOT forward (causes thin shots)
  • Weight Distribution: Keep weight forward (60/40) - don't lean back!
  • Swing Path: Make normal descending swing
  • Trust the Loft: Don't try to scoop or help ball up - let club do the work
  • Follow-through: Complete finish with full release
Common Mistake: Leaning back and trying to lift the ball. This causes fat and thin shots. Keep weight forward and trust your lob wedge's loft to get the ball high quickly.

Characteristics

  • Definition: Normal ball flight produced by proper contact with pitching or gap wedge
  • Flight Path: Moderate height with balanced carry and roll
  • Landing Behavior: One or two hops, then releases toward hole
  • Versatility: Works in most conditions and situations
  • Control: Easiest trajectory to execute consistently

Setup for Standard Flight

  • Ball Position: Center of stance
  • Stance Width: Narrow stance - feet inside shoulders
  • Weight: Slightly forward (55/45)
  • Hands: Neutral to slightly forward at address
  • Club Selection: Pitching wedge (46-48°) or Gap wedge (50-52°)

When to Use

  • Default Choice: When no special circumstances require high or low shot
  • Ample Green: Plenty of landing area and room for roll
  • Calm Conditions: No significant wind to fight
  • Level Lie: Flat fairway or first cut of rough
  • Practice Preference: Most-practiced trajectory = highest consistency
Key Point: Master the standard trajectory before experimenting with high/low shots. This is your bread-and-butter pitch that should work 80% of the time.

When to Use

  • Strong Headwind: Keep ball low to combat wind
  • Under Tree Branches: Need to keep ball below obstacles
  • Firm Greens: Hard surfaces favor running approach
  • Downhill Green: Landing on downslope - need roll to reach hole
  • Links-Style Play: Running shots are safer in firm, fast conditions

Setup Adjustments

  • Club Up: Use gap wedge or pitching wedge (less loft) instead of sand wedge
  • Narrow Stance: Feet closer together for control
  • Ball Position: Move ball back in stance toward right foot
  • Grip Down: Choke down 5-5 cm on club for control
  • Open Stance: Slightly open (left) to compensate for ball-back position
  • Weight Forward: 60/40 or more favoring front foot
  • Hands Pressed Forward: Hands well ahead of ball at address and impact

Swing Technique

  • Shorter Backswing: Controlled, compact motion
  • Descending Blow: Hit down on ball, taking divot after contact
  • Low Follow-through: Keep hands low through finish - don't release fully
  • Firm Wrists: Less wrist hinge than normal pitch
  • Body Rotation: Turn through shot but keep follow-through abbreviated

Expected Results

  • Flight Path: Penetrating, low trajectory
  • Landing: Ball lands hot with forward momentum
  • Roll: Expect 2-3x more roll than carry
  • Spin: Reduced backspin compared to standard pitch
  • Landing Spot: Must account for significant roll - land well short of hole
Important Caveat: Don't use low running pitch if hazards (bunkers, water) exist between landing spot and hole. Ball will roll significantly more than normal.

What is a Flop Shot?

  • Definition: Very high shot over very short distance
  • Objective: Ball lands softly and stops almost immediately
  • Trajectory: Maximum height with minimal distance
  • Risk Level: High - requires precise execution
  • Alternative Name: Also called "lob shot"

When to Attempt

  • Close to Green: Standing near green with obstacle between you and flag
  • Over Bunker: Must clear greenside bunker with tight pin position
  • Fast Greens: Very fast surfaces where ball would run too far
  • Tight Pin: Flag close to your side with little green to work with
  • Good Lie: Fluffy lie or clean fairway - NOT tight lie

Basic Setup

  • Club: Lob wedge (60° or higher) is essential
  • Face Open: Open clubface significantly at address
  • Stance Wide: Wider stance than normal pitch for stability
  • Weight Back: Can move weight back slightly (unique to flop shot)
  • Ball Forward: Position ball forward in stance
  • Lie Quality: Fluffy lie gives confidence; tight lie is risky

Execution Technique

  • Open Clubface: Face points right of target at address
  • Swing Path: Swing along body line (left of target)
  • Aggressive Swing: Full, confident swing (don't decelerate!)
  • Slide Under Ball: Club slides under ball without taking divot
  • High Finish: Complete follow-through above shoulders
Risk Factors: Flop shot is high-risk. Possible mistakes include thinning over green, fatting short of target, or catching ball with leading edge. Only attempt when necessary and when you have good lie.
Practice First: Master standard pitches before attempting flops. Practice flop shots extensively on range before trying on course. Have a backup plan ready.

Quick Fixes

Having trouble? Identify your issue and find the solution.

Causes

  • Deceleration: Slowing down through impact instead of accelerating
  • Too Short Backswing: Not enough swing length for distance needed
  • Wrong Club: Using too much loft for the distance
  • Lack of Commitment: Tentative swing with doubt

Fixes

  • Accelerate Through: Feel club speed increasing through impact, not slowing
  • Longer Backswing: Take bigger backswing if consistently short
  • Club Down: Use less lofted wedge (gap wedge instead of sand wedge)
  • Commit Fully: Trust your club choice and make confident swing
  • Follow-through: Ensure complete finish - don't quit on shot

Practice Drill

Acceleration Drill:

  1. Place ball 25m from target
  2. Make practice swing focusing on acceleration through impact
  3. Feel club speed increasing from backswing to follow-through
  4. Hit ball with same accelerating feeling
  5. Repeat until consistent distance control achieved

Causes

  • Too Much Club: Using club with too little loft for distance
  • Aggressive Swing: Swinging too hard, adding unintended distance
  • Thin Contact: Catching ball on upswing or with leading edge
  • Too Long Backswing: Swing length excessive for distance needed

Fixes

  • More Loft: Use higher lofted wedge (sand wedge or lob wedge)
  • Smooth Tempo: Control distance with swing length, not speed
  • Shorten Backswing: Reduce backswing length for less distance
  • Weight Forward: Keep weight on front foot to avoid thin contact
  • Ball Position: Center or slightly back to ensure solid contact

Practice Drill

Swing Length Drill:

  1. Select target 35m away
  2. Hit 5 balls with hip-high backswing
  3. Hit 5 balls with chest-high backswing
  4. Note which swing length gives correct distance
  5. Use that swing length as your reference for this yardage

Causes

  • Rising Through Impact: Standing up or straightening spine during swing
  • Weight Hanging Back: Weight stays on back foot through impact
  • Ball Too Far Forward: Ball position too far left in stance
  • Scooping Motion: Trying to help ball up with hands/wrists
  • Poor Posture: Not maintaining spine angle through swing

Fixes

  • Stay Down: Maintain spine angle through impact and into follow-through
  • Weight Forward: Transfer weight to front foot, keep it there
  • Ball Position: Move ball to center or slightly back in stance
  • Descending Blow: Hit down on ball, trust loft to create height
  • Steady Head: Keep head position stable, don't lift up

Practice Drill

Divot After Ball Drill:

  1. Draw line in sand or on grass
  2. Place ball just behind (target-side) of line
  3. Make swing trying to take divot after line, not before
  4. Successful swing = ball first, then divot past line
  5. Practice until you consistently take divot in correct spot

Causes

  • Weight on Back Foot: Hanging back instead of shifting forward
  • Early Release: Releasing club too soon, wrists unhinging early
  • Ball Too Far Back: Excessively back position increases ground contact
  • Casting Motion: Throwing club at ball from top of swing
  • Bottom of Arc Too Early: Swing reaches low point before ball

Fixes

  • Weight Transfer: Shift weight decisively to front foot during downswing
  • Hands Lead: Keep hands ahead of clubhead through impact zone
  • Ball Position: Move ball to center of stance
  • Late Release: Maintain wrist hinge longer into downswing
  • Quiet Lower Body: Reduce excessive leg movement that shifts swing bottom

Practice Drill

Tee Drill for Clean Contact:

  1. Push tee into ground leaving 1/10 cm above surface
  2. Place ball on top of tee
  3. Make pitch swing trying to clip ball off tee
  4. Goal: Hit ball cleanly without hitting tee into ground
  5. Success = clean contact; failure = hitting behind and driving tee down

Causes

  • Variable Swing Length: Inconsistent backswing and follow-through lengths
  • Changing Tempo: Sometimes quick, sometimes slow
  • Contact Quality: Mix of thin, fat, and solid contacts
  • Wrong Focus: Thinking about mechanics instead of target
  • Club Selection Guess: Not knowing your wedge distances

Fixes

  • Reference Points: Establish hip-high, chest-high, shoulder-high as your swing lengths
  • Constant Tempo: Use same rhythm for all pitches (practice with metronome)
  • Improve Contact: Work on solid ball-first contact every time
  • Target Focus: Look at landing spot, swing to target (not at mechanics)
  • Know Your Numbers: Practice to establish your exact yardages for each swing length

Practice Drill

Distance Mapping Drill:

  1. Take sand wedge to practice range
  2. Hit 10 balls with quarter swing (hip-high): measure average distance
  3. Hit 10 balls with half swing (chest-high): measure average distance
  4. Hit 10 balls with three-quarter swing (shoulder-high): measure average
  5. Record these three distances - these are YOUR reference yardages
  6. Repeat with gap wedge and pitching wedge
  7. Now you have a complete distance chart for all wedges and swing lengths
Keep a Yardage Card: Write down your wedge distances and keep in golf bag. Reference before each pitch shot to eliminate guesswork.
