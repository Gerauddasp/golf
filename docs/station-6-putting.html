Distance control + short putt confidence

Quick Reference

Putt Type Focus Key
Short (0-2m) Make it Confident stroke, listen for drop
Medium (2-6m) Speed + Line Pick spot, commit to line
Lag (6m+) Speed only Get within 1m circle
The One Rule: Speed is more important than line. A putt with good speed and average line beats perfect line with poor speed.

The Two Skills

Short Putts Lag Putts
Goal Make it Inside 1m
Focus Line first Speed first
Stroke Firm, confident Smooth, feel distance
Eyes Stay down, listen Watch ball roll
Break Play less Speed determines break
Key difference: Short putts need confidence. Lag putts need feel.

Green Reading Basics

Situation What to Do
Overall Slope Find the low side first - water drains there
Speed & Break Faster putts break less, slower break more
Uphill Be aggressive, play LESS break
Downhill Focus on speed, play MORE break

Practice Exercises

Clock Drill
SetupTees at 1m around hole (12, 3, 6, 9 o'clock)
RepsMake from all 4 positions
Goal4/4 before moving to 1.5m
Success: Build confidence at scoring distances with different breaks
Lag to Circle
SetupTees in 1m circle around hole, putt from 6-9m
Reps10 putts
Goal8/10 inside the circle
Success: Eliminate three-putts by mastering distance control
Up and Down Challenge
SetupLag putt from 6m+, then finish
Reps9 putts from different spots
GoalZero three-putts (9/9 two-putt or better)
Note: This is the most important putting skill. Three-putts kill scores.

Quick Fixes

Having trouble? Tap to see solutions.

Cause: Shoulder alignment, path issues, or face angle at impact

Fix:

  1. Check shoulder alignment - should be parallel to target line
  2. Eyes directly over the ball
  3. Practice Putting Gate drill with alignment sticks
  4. Lighten grip pressure - tension causes pushing

Cause: Deceleration, backswing too long, fear of going past

Fix:

  1. SHORTEN backswing - then accelerate through
  2. Follow-through longer than backswing
  3. Practice rule: any putt stopping short = move ball back 15cm
  4. Ideal speed: ball finishes 30cm past hole on a miss

Cause: Peeking too soon, rushing routine, timid stroke

Fix:

  1. Listen for the drop - don't look up until you hear it
  2. Same routine every time, even for short putts
  3. Pick specific spot at back of cup - be precise
  4. Confident stroke - short putts need firm commitment
