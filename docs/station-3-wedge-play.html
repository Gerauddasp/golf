Session Goal

Develop distance control and trajectory management with wedges

Focus on consistent yardages with each wedge (PW, GW, SW, LW) and learn to shape trajectory for different situations.

Suggested Practice Session (45 minutes)

  1. Warm-up (5 min): Easy half-swings with sand wedge
  2. Know Your Distances (10 min): 10 balls each with SW, GW, PW - note carry distances
  3. Clock System (15 min): Practice 9:00, 8:00, and 7:00 positions with one wedge
  4. Trajectory Control (10 min): 5 high shots, 5 low shots alternating
  5. Target Practice (5 min): Pick random yardage, hit shot with appropriate club/swing length

Track your progress: Note which distances feel most comfortable and which need more work.

Full Swing Wedge Drills

Drill: Establish your baseline full-swing distance for each wedge.

Setup:

  • Hit 10 balls with each wedge at full swing
  • Note the carry distance (ignore outliers)
  • Calculate your average for each club

Typical Ranges:

  • Pitching Wedge (PW): 110-130 yards
  • Gap Wedge (GW): 95-110 yards
  • Sand Wedge (SW): 80-95 yards
  • Lob Wedge (LW): 65-80 yards
Important: These are typical distance ranges. Your actual distances depend on swing speed, loft, shaft, and conditions. Practice to determine your personal yardage gaps.

Success Metric:

8 out of 10 shots within 5 yards of your average distance for each club.

Note: Your distances may vary - the key is knowing YOUR numbers with confidence.

Purpose: Master the critical 90-yard distance that comes up frequently on the course.

Technique:

  • Backswing: Stop at 3/4 length (hands to shoulder height)
  • Tempo: Smooth and controlled, not rushed
  • Follow-through: Balanced finish, chest facing target
  • Club selection: Usually gap wedge or sand wedge with controlled swing

Drill Progression:

  1. 10 practice swings feeling the 3/4 length
  2. Hit 5 balls focusing only on swing length consistency
  3. Hit 10 balls aiming for 90-yard target
  4. Measure dispersion - goal is 8/10 within 10 yards of target

Key Feel:

"Same setup as full swing, shorten the turn, keep the tempo"

Purpose: Develop feel for the half-swing distance, essential for approach shots.

Setup:

  • Ball position: Center of stance
  • Stance: Slightly narrower than full swing
  • Weight: 60% on front foot
  • Hands: Slightly forward of ball

Swing Keys:

  • Backswing: Hands to hip height (parallel to ground)
  • Body rotation: Controlled shoulder turn, quiet lower body
  • Contact: Ball first, crisp divot after
  • Finish: Abbreviated but balanced

Practice Routine:

  1. 5 practice swings with focus on hip-height backswing
  2. Hit 15 balls with sand wedge or lob wedge
  3. Track carry distance for each shot

Success Metric:

10 out of 15 shots between 70-80 yards with consistent trajectory.

Purpose: Master the delicate 60-yard distance where feel and touch are critical.

Technique:

  • Setup: Narrow stance, weight forward (70/30)
  • Backswing: Short and controlled (hands to waist height)
  • Acceleration: Smooth through impact, don't decelerate
  • Club choice: Lob wedge or sand wedge with abbreviated swing

Clock System Reference:

  • 7:00 to 5:00: Approximately 60 yards
  • Adjust for conditions: Wind, lie, green firmness

Drill:

  1. Place target at 60 yards
  2. Hit 20 balls alternating between SW and LW
  3. Note which club gives you better feel and consistency
  4. Stick with that club for this distance going forward

Success Metric:

12 out of 20 shots within 15 feet of target (5-yard circle).

Key: Consistent tempo and acceleration through the ball - never quit on the shot.

Concept: Use clock positions to create consistent, repeatable backswing lengths.

Clock Positions (Right-Handed Golfer):

  • 9:00 to 3:00: Full swing (parallel to parallel)
  • 8:00 to 4:00: 3/4 swing (~90-100 yards)
  • 7:00 to 5:00: 1/2 swing (~60-75 yards)
  • 6:30 to 5:30: 1/3 swing (~40-50 yards)

Practice Drill:

  1. Stand in front of mirror or use video
  2. Practice each clock position 10 times
  3. Visualize clock face as you swing
  4. Hit 5 balls at each position
  5. Record your distance for each position with each wedge

Trajectory Control

When to use: Tight pin positions, firm greens, need to stop the ball quickly, obstacle to clear.

Setup Adjustments:

  • Ball position: Forward in stance (inside left heel)
  • Stance: Slightly open (align left of target)
  • Weight: Balanced or slightly favoring back foot
  • Clubface: Open 5-10 degrees at address
  • Hands: Even with or slightly behind ball (not forward)

Swing Keys:

  • Tempo: Smooth and controlled, no aggressive hit
  • Path: Swing along body line (slightly out-to-in)
  • Release: Allow club to release fully, don't hold off
  • Finish: High hands, full finish

Practice Drill:

  1. Start with lob wedge (most loft)
  2. Hit 10 balls with high trajectory focus
  3. Observe apex height and landing softness
  4. Progress to sand wedge and gap wedge

Success Metric:

Ball lands softly with minimal roll (2-3 yards maximum).

When to use: Windy conditions, running shot onto green, need extra roll, punch out from trouble.

Setup Adjustments:

  • Ball position: Back in stance (center or back of center)
  • Stance: Narrow, slightly closed stance
  • Weight: 70% on front foot
  • Hands: Well ahead of ball at address
  • Shaft lean: Significant forward lean (delofts club)

Swing Keys:

  • Backswing: Shorter, more controlled (3/4 length max)
  • Downswing: Hands lead, maintain forward shaft lean
  • Impact: Ball-first contact, crisp divot
  • Finish: Abbreviated, low hands, chest toward target

Club Selection:

Consider using pitching wedge or gap wedge instead of sand/lob wedge to reduce trajectory naturally.

Practice Drill:

  1. Set up obstacle at waist height, 10 yards in front
  2. Practice hitting under the obstacle
  3. Hit 15 balls focusing on low trajectory
  4. Note how much further the ball rolls vs. high shot

Success Metric:

Ball flight peaks at 10-15 feet max, rolls out 10-15 yards after landing.

Purpose: Understand what creates spin and how to control it.

Factors That Create Backspin:

  • Clean contact: Ball-first, then turf
  • Clubhead speed: Faster speed = more spin
  • Loft: More loft = more spin potential
  • Grooves: Clean, sharp grooves essential
  • Ball condition: Clean, quality ball spins more
  • Lie: Tight lies produce more spin than fluffy lies

How to Maximize Spin:

  1. Clean equipment: Wipe clubface and ball before shot
  2. Descending blow: Strike ball on downswing, take divot after
  3. Aggressive swing: Don't decelerate through impact
  4. Quality contact: Center of clubface, not toe or heel
  5. Square to slightly open face: Avoid closing clubface

How to Reduce Spin (When Needed):

  • Use less lofted club (PW instead of SW)
  • Ball position slightly back
  • Hands forward, deloft the club
  • Shallower angle of attack
  • Softer swing speed

Practice Drill:

  1. Hit 10 balls with focus on maximum spin
  2. Hit 10 balls with focus on minimum spin
  3. Compare landing and roll behavior

Note: Realistic expectations - amateur golfers typically achieve 4,000-7,000 rpm with wedges. Tour pros can exceed 10,000 rpm with perfect conditions.

Shot Shaping with Wedges

When to use: Pin tucked on left side, right-to-left wind, need extra roll after landing.

Setup:

  • Alignment: Aim body and feet right of target
  • Clubface: Square to target (closed relative to stance)
  • Ball position: Center or slightly back of center
  • Grip: Standard or slightly stronger grip

Swing Path:

  • Swing along body line (in-to-out path)
  • Allow clubface to close through impact
  • Full release of hands and forearms
  • Feel right hand rolling over left after impact

Ball Flight:

Ball starts right of target, curves back left. Lands with more forward roll than straight shot.

Practice Drill:

  1. Set alignment stick on ground pointing right of target
  2. Align body parallel to stick
  3. Set clubface square to actual target
  4. Hit 10 balls focusing on swing path along body line

Success Metric:

8 out of 10 balls start right and curve back toward target.

When to use: Pin tucked on right side, left-to-right wind, need softer landing with less roll.

Setup:

  • Alignment: Aim body and feet left of target
  • Clubface: Square to target (open relative to stance)
  • Ball position: Slightly forward of center
  • Grip: Standard or slightly weaker grip
  • Stance: Can be slightly wider for stability

Swing Path:

  • Swing along body line (out-to-in path)
  • Hold clubface slightly open through impact
  • Feel like you're cutting across the ball
  • Abbreviated finish, don't roll hands over

Ball Flight:

Ball starts left of target, curves back right. Higher trajectory, softer landing, minimal roll.

Practice Drill:

  1. Set alignment stick pointing left of target
  2. Align body parallel to stick
  3. Set clubface square to actual target
  4. Hit 10 balls with out-to-in path

Success Metric:

8 out of 10 balls start left and curve back toward target with controlled trajectory.

Strategic Considerations:

Best Times to Shape Wedge Shots:

  • Tucked pins: Curve ball toward pin from safe angle
  • Wind play: Use curve to hold ball against wind
  • Green slope: Land on high side, let slope feed ball to pin
  • Trouble avoidance: Curve away from hazards
  • Roll control: Draw for extra roll, fade to check up

When NOT to Shape:

  • Under pressure - go with your natural shot
  • Haven't practiced the shot recently
  • Straight shot is perfectly fine option
  • Conditions (lie, wind) make shaping unpredictable

Practice Recommendation:

Spend 80% of practice on straight shots, 20% on shaping. Shape shots are tools for specific situations, not your default approach.

On-Course Decision Matrix:

  1. Identify target and optimal landing area
  2. Assess if straight shot works well
  3. If yes - hit straight shot with confidence
  4. If no - consider which shape provides best miss
  5. Execute shape only if practiced and confident

Having Trouble? Quick Fixes

Common Causes:

  • Rising up through impact (loss of posture)
  • Weight hanging back on trail foot
  • Trying to "scoop" or help ball up
  • Decelerating through impact

Quick Fix:

  1. Weight forward: 60-70% on front foot at setup, stay there
  2. Hands forward: Ensure hands ahead of ball at address and impact
  3. Stay down: Maintain spine angle through impact - don't stand up
  4. Accelerate: Swing through the ball, not at it

Drill:

Coin Drill: Place coin 2 inches in front of ball. Goal is to hit the coin after hitting the ball. Forces you to stay down and through impact.

Success Check:

Taking divot after ball, solid contact, ball launches with proper trajectory.

Common Causes:

  • Weight falling back onto trail foot in downswing
  • Releasing club too early (casting)
  • Ball position too far forward
  • Trying to "scoop" ball into air

Quick Fix:

  1. Ball position: Move ball back to center of stance
  2. Weight shift: Feel 70% weight on front foot through entire swing
  3. Hands lead: Keep hands ahead of clubhead through impact
  4. Chest forward: Chest over ball at impact, not behind

Drill:

Front-Foot-Only Drill: Hit 10 balls standing only on front foot. Forces proper weight distribution and prevents falling back.

Practice Progression:

  1. 10 swings front-foot-only (no ball)
  2. 10 balls front-foot-only
  3. 10 balls normal stance, maintaining same feeling

Common Causes:

  • Varying backswing length
  • Inconsistent tempo/rhythm
  • Poor contact location on clubface
  • Changing setup from shot to shot

Quick Fix:

  1. Standardize backswing: Use clock system (9:00, 8:00, 7:00 positions)
  2. Constant tempo: Same rhythm for all distances - only vary backswing length
  3. Center contact: Focus on middle of clubface every shot
  4. Pre-shot routine: Same setup process every time

Drill:

Three-Distance Drill:

  1. Pick three target distances (50, 70, 90 yards)
  2. Assign clock position to each distance
  3. Hit 5 balls to each target using only your clock position (don't think about distance)
  4. Measure dispersion
  5. Goal: 4/5 balls within 10 yards of target at each distance

Key Principle:

"Swing length controls distance, not swing speed. Same tempo, different lengths."

Potential Causes:

  • Worn grooves on wedges
  • Dirty clubface or ball
  • Sweeping contact instead of descending blow
  • Decelerating through impact
  • Poor lie (grass between ball and clubface)

Equipment Check:

  1. Groove condition: Replace wedges every 50-75 rounds
  2. Clean clubface: Use brush/towel before every shot
  3. Ball quality: Use premium balls with urethane cover

Technique Fix:

  1. Steeper attack: Ball position center or back, hands forward
  2. Accelerate through: Never decelerate - swing through the ball
  3. Crisp contact: Ball first, then divot - no grass between
  4. Square to open face: Don't close clubface at impact

Drill:

Divot Direction Drill:

  • Hit 20 wedge shots focusing on crisp divots pointing at target
  • Divot should start after ball position
  • Deeper divot = more descending blow = more spin

Realistic Expectations:

From fairway lies with premium equipment and good technique, expect:

  • Full swing from 80+ yards: Ball may spin and check
  • Partial swings 40-70 yards: Minimal spin, some roll
  • From rough: "Flyer" lie reduces spin significantly

Remember: Distance control comes from consistent swing length and tempo, not swing speed.
