Master ball-first contact and consistent striking with irons

Downswing Drills

Purpose

Transfer weight properly onto the front foot and avoid reverse pivot errors.

Setup

Take your normal address position with an iron.

Execution

  • During downswing initiation, push on your right toes
  • This causes your right heel to lift naturally
  • Feel your weight shift dynamically to your front foot
  • Complete the swing with weight firmly on left side

Goal

Prevent weight from staying stuck on back foot, which causes weak contact and fat shots.

Purpose

Eliminate over-the-top swing errors causing outside-in swing paths.

Setup

  • Grip the shaft below the normal grip with your right hand
  • Take backswing to top position

Key Focus

  • From the top, drop your right elbow straight down toward right side of hips
  • Feel the elbow move downward, not forward
  • This shallows the club path automatically

Common Error Prevented

Over-the-top (casting the club from outside), which causes slices and pulls.

Goal

Promote inside-to-square club delivery for solid, powerful contact.

Impact Drills

Purpose

Strengthen the left side for more power in golf shots.

Key Movements

  • Bump hips forward during transition from backswing
  • Straighten the left knee fully through impact
  • Rotate hips and shoulders through the downswing
  • Feel your left leg become a solid post

Benefit

Creates a strong left side "post" to swing against for maximum power transfer. This is how the pros generate incredible clubhead speed.

What It Feels Like

You should feel your weight firmly on your left leg at impact, with your left side braced and solid.

Source: Golf Distillery - Impact Drills

Purpose

Develop powerful impact mechanics by practicing with a weighted training bag.

Training Aid Required

Impact bag (weighted practice aid)

Critical Focus Areas

  • Maintain lag in wrists: Avoid early flicking or release
  • Transfer weight onto front foot: Feel 80-90% of weight on left side
  • Hands ahead of clubhead: Forward shaft lean at impact
  • Hips open to target: Not square or closed

Execution

Take half to three-quarter swings into the bag, focusing on proper impact position without worrying about follow-through.

Benefit

Provides physical feedback for proper lag retention and weight transfer. You'll immediately feel when your impact position is weak or strong.

Source: Golf Distillery - Impact Drills

Purpose

Build impact strength and identify optimal positioning through resistance.

Setup

Use a heavy object to press against (bag, wall, or training aid)

Execution

  • Position yourself in your impact position
  • Press the clubhead into the heavy object
  • Push as hard as you can, feeling which position generates most power

Key Observations for Maximum Power

  • Weight transfers to left foot
  • Arms fully extend through impact zone
  • Wrists stay forward ahead of ball position
  • Hips rotated open to target
  • Left leg straightened (but not locked)

Benefit

Develops strength and body awareness for powerful impact positions. This drill reveals your optimal impact mechanics through feel.

Release Drills

Purpose

Correct under-rotation of hands and arms near impact.

Key Focus

  • Right arm rolls aggressively over the left through impact
  • Maintain active rotation through extension phase
  • Prevent lazy arm action
  • Feel the forearms rotating, not just the hands

Execution

  • Make half swings focusing solely on arm rotation
  • Watch your forearms: right should rotate over left post-impact
  • The back of your left hand should face the ground in follow-through

Common Errors Prevented

  • Slices (from open clubface)
  • Pushes (from blocked release)
  • Weak, short shots (from no compression)

Benefit

Trains proper forearm rotation for square clubface delivery and maximum distance.

Source: Golf Distillery - Release Drills

Purpose

Train hands and wrists to remain active through the release.

Key Checkpoint

  • Clubface becomes perpendicular to ground after impact
  • Hands actively rotate (not passive)
  • Wrists stay engaged through the ball
  • No "chicken wing" with left elbow

Execution

  • Take slow-motion swings
  • Stop in your follow-through when arms are parallel to ground
  • Check: Is the clubface perpendicular to ground?
  • If clubface is still open, increase hand rotation

Common Error Prevented

Lazy hand action that leaves the clubface open at impact, causing slices and weak contact.

Benefit

Develops active hand release for consistent ball flight and direction control.

Quick Fixes - Having Trouble?

Diagnosis

Club penetrates the ground before making contact with the ball. This dramatically reduces distance and produces a heavy, chunked feel.

Root Cause

The bottom of your swing arc is located behind the ball (too early) or too low into the ground.

Common Causes

  • Swaying: Moving hips laterally during backswing
  • Failing to maintain spine angle: Upper body moves forward at impact
  • Ball too far forward: In stance
  • Stance too wide: Lowers swing arc
  • Weight on back foot: At address or impact

Quick Fixes

  1. Keep right knee flexed: Lean right knee inward slightly to prevent swaying
  2. Maintain spine angle: Imagine head pressed against wall behind you from address through impact
  3. Center the ball: For mid-irons, position ball in center of stance
  4. Weight forward: Distribute weight evenly or slightly favor front foot
  5. Narrow stance: Feet should align with outside of shoulders

Recommended Drill

Weight Transfer Drill (Downswing #1) - Push on right toes during downswing to ensure proper weight shift.

Source: Golf Distillery - How to Fix Fat Shots

Diagnosis

Leading edge hits ball below its equator instead of sweet spot. Ball flies lower than normal and rolls excessively. Little or no divot produced.

Root Cause

Bottom of swing arc is either too high above ground or positioned incorrectly relative to ball.

Common Causes

  • Standing up at impact: Raising the swing arc
  • Swaying: Moving swing arc from its setup position
  • Reverse pivot: Weight stays on front foot during backswing
  • Flipping/Scooping: Hands stop leading, bringing swing arc back
  • Ball too far forward: Asking body to reach for it

Quick Fixes

  1. Keep head in the box: Maintain same vertical distance from ground at impact as address
  2. Lean right knee inward: Prevents swaying during backswing
  3. Pivot toward back foot: Proper weight transfer during backswing
  4. Don't release wrist hinge: Keep angle through impact, release in follow-through
  5. Adjust ball position: Center for wedges, gradually forward for longer clubs

Recommended Drill

Anti Over-the-Top Drill (Downswing #3) - Promotes proper path and contact.

Source: Golf Distillery - How to Fix Thin Shots

Diagnosis

Ball starts right of target and continues straight on that line without curving (for right-handed golfers).

Root Cause

Inside-out club path with clubface open to target but square to path. Both path and face point right.

Common Causes

  • Aligned right of target: Setup issue
  • Inside-out swing with weak grip: Path + lazy release
  • Blocking shots: Not finishing swing, leaving face open
  • Holding club too tightly: Restricts release

Quick Fixes

  1. Check alignment: Clubface aims at target, body/feet parallel to that line
  2. Take club back square: Not inside - aim for inside-square-inside path
  3. Strengthen grip: See 2-3 knuckles on left hand at address
  4. Finish the swing: Commit fully, don't "block" or quit on it
  5. Lighter grip pressure: Especially right hand - allows natural release

Recommended Drill

Proper Arm Rotation Drill (Release #1) - Trains active release preventing blocked, pushed shots.

Source: Golf Distillery - How to Fix Push Shots

Diagnosis

Ball starts left of target and continues straight on that line without curving (for right-handed golfers).

Root Cause

Outside-in club path with clubface closed to target but square to path. Clubface and path both point left.

Common Causes

  • Aligned left of target: Setup issue
  • Outside-in path with strong grip: Path + active release closes face
  • Over-the-top swing: Clubhead outside target line on downswing

Quick Fixes

  1. Adopt square stance: Verify you're aimed at target correctly, not left
  2. Correct swing path: Transform outside-in to inside-square-inside
  3. Square takeaway: Not outside the line during first move
  4. Weaken grip: See only 1-2 knuckles on left hand (vs. 3+)

Recommended Drill

Anti Over-the-Top Drill (Downswing #3) - Corrects outside-in path that causes pulls.

Source: Golf Distillery - How to Fix Pull Shots

Diagnosis

Your irons aren't traveling as far as they used to, or you're coming up short on approach shots consistently.

Common Causes

  • Early release: Casting or flipping loses lag and power
  • Poor weight transfer: Weight stays on back foot
  • No compression: Not trapping ball against turf
  • Lack of rotation: All arms, no body turn
  • Weak impact position: Hands not ahead, no shaft lean

Quick Fixes

  1. Maintain lag: Keep wrists cocked longer, don't release early
  2. Weight forward: 80-90% on left side at impact
  3. Hands ahead: Forward shaft lean compresses the ball
  4. Rotate through: Hips and shoulders turn toward target
  5. Accelerate through ball: Never decelerate before impact

Recommended Drills

  • Impact Bag Drill (Impact #2): Builds power through proper impact mechanics
  • Snap Left Knee Drill (Impact #1): Creates solid post for power transfer
